# ANTAGONISTS & OPPOSITION
### GM only — how to structure adversity in the Veilwrack

*The Veilwrack has no Dark Lord. Its opposition is layered: a dying world at
the bottom, institutions defending a lie in the middle, and ordinary
predators, rivals, and zealots moving through both. Run opposition as five
tiers, escalating as the campaign arc advances.*

---

## Tier 0 — The World Itself (ambient, every session)

The environment is the ever-present antagonist and should claim more rolls
than any enemy.

- **Altitude & cold:** off-lane flight in the Reach costs Fatigue;
  Glare-edge work requires Endurance rolls; a soaked Alar in cold air is in
  Survival-roll territory within the hour.
- **Weather:** storm-cells, shear between altitude bands, the seasonal High
  Gales. A storm is best run as a skill gauntlet: Navigation → Flight →
  Brawn (hold the cargo) → Flight, with Fatigue as the cost of failure
  rather than instant death.
- **Falls:** the signature dread. Remember the racial ability: a Flight
  roll halves falling distance, a critical arrests it. Wing wounds turn
  every fight at altitude into a potential fall — use it, but telegraph it.
- **The Stillings:** the world-antagonist. Use the GM reference in
  gm-secrets.md (Flight Hard per round, hearing Herculean, no Windworking,
  Quieting after 24h). A Stilling is a *place that hunts by waiting*. The
  horror beats: sound dying mid-sentence; lanterns going blue; the moment
  the players realize the exit bearing is a guess.

## Tier 1 — Predators & Creatures (early sessions)

Stat blocks in bestiary.md / DB templates.

- **Stillwights** — ambush horror inside and near dead air. Tactics: silent
  approach (nothing to hear anyway), grapple, *drink the breath* (Endurance
  contests). Never field more than the party can flee.
- **Sky-drakes** — apex pursuit predators of the Shade; attack barges and
  stragglers. An aerial chase set-piece, not a toe-to-toe fight.
- **Murk-things** (undescribed, deliberately) — whatever lives in the
  Undermist's skim. Tentacled implications, glimpses only, until Act 4.
- **Mundane dangers:** krill-swarm blinding clouds, reef-bone collapses,
  rabid gleaner-flocks. Cheap, fast encounters that keep flying dangerous.

## Tier 2 — Human Adversaries (the social middle game)

- **The Lanhawk clutches** — windlane pirates out of the Veyl Reefs.
  Structure: independent clutches of 6–20 under a Talon-captain; loose
  council (the **Mews**) that meets to divide territories. Useful because
  they are *negotiable* — Lanhawks honor shrine-ground and ransom custom.
  Recurring villain: **Talon Serra Redjess**, who holds a grudge like an
  heirloom and a genuine grievance: her home spire was stripped by Combine
  crews under Crown charter, and she has decided the lawful world owes her
  a living. She should be fought twice, bargained with once, and available
  as a desperate ally by Act 4.
- **Crown Agents** — the antagonists of the archive chapters. They are not
  thugs; they are *editors*. Their weapons: confiscation, charter revocation,
  arrest of allies, the offer (always sincere, always poisoned) of
  legitimacy in exchange for silence. Recurring face: **Quiet Plume Avess**,
  courteous, tired, increasingly aware she is suppressing the truth about
  the thing that will kill her grandchildren. Convertible — at a price.
- **Combine muscle** — Baron's Hands and bought magistrates when the PCs
  pull at the sink-data thread. The Combine escalates economically first
  (debts called in, guild access revoked, barge embargo on the PCs' home
  roost) and violently only off the books.
- **Choir zealots** — outer-cult Singers who decide the PCs are profaning
  the holy quiet. Dangerous precisely because they know Stilling-craft the
  PCs don't, and because killing sad, sincere pilgrims should feel terrible.

## Tier 3 — Institutional Antagonism (the long middle)

The campaign's true middle-game opposition is **the three founding
institutions defending the secret**, each differently:

| Institution | Method | Pressure point |
|---|---|---|
| Spirarchy | law, sedition charges, sealed archives | Velute will trade truth-management for genuine help containing panic |
| Quillate | denial of access, professional ruin, the Record itself | Orrocan's terror of the self-filling stacks; he *wants* to be forced |
| Deepway | silence, refusal of rites, immovable custom | the deadline — the Synod knows time is shorter than secrecy |

Run these as **escalating clocks, not combats.** Each institution has a
disposition track toward the party (Hostile → Obstructive → Neutral →
Complicit → Allied); major story beats move it. The campaign's design
intends all three to end somewhere past Neutral by Act 5 — the finale is
unplayable as written if the PCs have made permanent enemies of all three,
and that too is fair: then the finale becomes a heist against the whole
world, which is also a campaign.

## Tier 4 — The Mythic Antagonist (Acts 4–5)

The final opposition is arithmetic: **the Breath is running out, and waking
an Unsung costs more than anyone wants to pay.** The "antagonist" of the
finale is whoever the PCs decide must not pay it — or must:

- **The Choir's endgame:** the Sotto Voce, learning what the Unsung are,
  splits. One faction (Hssh) wants to help. The other decides hatching a
  new Worldlung would *undo the holy quiet* — and works sincerely toward
  the world's death. This is where the campaign's sympathetic cult finally
  yields true believers worth fighting.
- **The Combine's endgame:** a bottled spire's marrow can wake an Unsung.
  Hollow Vesse *is* a bottled spire. The Combine will fight to the last
  hired wing to keep its refinery from becoming the world's ransom — unless
  the PCs make Brell Coinfeather's insurance copy of the numbers public at
  exactly the right moment.
- **The Spirarchy's endgame:** Velute will offer the cruelest clean
  solution: requisition the Spindles — ten thousand unrecorded lungs the
  Record would never miss. She will call it the Settling Right. The PCs
  should hear the offer in a quiet room, politely, with tea.

## Structuring Opposition: Practical Rules

1. **One tier per spotlight.** Mix at most two tiers per session; rotate.
   Environmental + predator early; social + institutional in the middle;
   mythic choices late.
2. **Clocks over plots.** Track: each named Stilling's growth (100m/season,
   doubling per resident Hushed); each institution's disposition; Serra
   Redjess's grudge; the un-buried dead crisis; Hollow Vesse's sink-rate.
   Advance one or two visibly every session. The world should feel like it
   is *losing altitude* whether or not the PCs act.
3. **Every villain is right about something.** Velute about panic, the
   Combine about dependence on lift, the Choir about the world needing to
   give something back, Serra about who paid for the lawful world. Let them
   say it well.
4. **The Hushed are never simply monsters.** Use Stillwights when you need
   a monster. A Hushed encounter should always contain one detail that is
   unbearably a person: the remembered name, the bread crumbs in a circle,
   the child who follows.
5. **Telegraph wing-loss, fall damage, and Quieting.** These are the
   setting's three irreversibilities. Players should always see them
   coming one decision away — and the decision should be real.
