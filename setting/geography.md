# THE VEILWRACK ATLAS
### Geography, Realms, and Power — a player-facing gazetteer

*The Quillate's standard chart of the world is a circle of windlanes drawn on
black paper, because most of the world is sky.*

---

## The Shape of the World

The Veilwrack is a band of habitable sky roughly four miles deep, wrapped
around a world no living Alar has ever touched. Everything an Alar will ever
stand on floats.

**The altitude bands**, as every fledgling learns them:

- **The Glare** (above ~5 miles): thin, frigid, brilliant air. Unbreathable
  for more than minutes. Only the high gales and the sun live there. Reckless
  Vael racers "kiss the Glare" as a rite of passage; some come back.
- **The Reach** (3–5 miles): the upper habitable band. Cold, clean, fast air.
  Spire crowns, watch-roosts, the great windlanes. Most civic life happens
  here. Breath-pressure is comfortable; fires burn honest orange.
- **The Shade** (1–3 miles): warm, wet, cloud-streaked air below the spire
  waists. Skyfarms, rain-catches, drift-krill swarms, and most of the world's
  food. Also most of the world's predators.
- **The Root-Dark** (0–1 mile above the Undermist): hot, heavy, dim. The
  dangling spire-roots, the Marrower works, the Ossuin descent-stations.
  Lanterns burn low and red. Sound carries strangely. Unlicensed descent
  below the root-line is a crime nearly everywhere.
- **The Undermist** (below): the cloud-sea. Golden by day, green-lit by
  night, lethal in ways the kindreds argue about. Only Ossuin death-divers
  go below the surface skim, and only briefly.

There are between sixty and ninety settled Spires, depending on what you
count as settled, threaded together by some forty charted windlanes. Beyond
the charted lanes is the **Wrack** proper — open sky, rogue weather, drifting
bone-reefs, and whatever the old charts mean by the marginal note *"no
returns."*

## The Great Polities

Power in the Veilwrack is power over three things: **lift** (marrow-breath
and spire integrity), **lanes** (who may ride which wind), and **the Record**
(who is owed what, and since when). Each great polity controls one and covets
the other two.

### The Crown Dominion (Suruveil's Crown and its client spires)

The largest state in the world: Suruveil's Crown itself (pop. ~200,000) plus
eleven client spires within two days' flight, bound by charters of fealty.
Governed by the **Spirarchy** — part monarchy, part church, part land
registry. The Spirarch (currently **Velute the Ninth**, Roak) rules from the
Tenth Gallery; beneath her, the **Gallery Lords** each administer one ring of
the Crown, from the glittering First Gallery (diplomacy, festivals, the
Quillate's halls) down to the smoke-dark Ninth (marrow-tapping, refuse,
debtors). Your gallery of residence is your legal class; moving up a gallery
takes a generation or a fortune.

The Spirarchy's legitimacy rests on the **Settling Right**: the doctrine that
the founding lineages were *granted* the Crown by the dying elder world, and
that order aloft descends from that grant. Question the Settling Right in
public and you will meet the Crown Agents.

**Power structure:** Spirarch → Gallery Lords (9) → Wardens-of-Ring (civic
magistrates) → roost-reeves (block-level headmen). Parallel to all of it: the
**Crown Agents**, answerable to the Spirarch alone.

### The Moult (the Vael raft-nation)

Not a place — a *fleet*. Two to four thousand kite-rafts, tether-barges, and
woven gliders, lashed into shifting neighborhoods, following the seasonal
gales. The Moult anchors off a major spire for a season at a time, trading,
hiring out couriers, and absorbing the restless young of every roost it
passes.

Governed by the **Raft-Council**: one speaker per raft-cluster, meeting in
the open air, decisions made by *lift* — councilors literally rise from their
perches to vote, and a motion passes when more wing-area is airborne for than
against. Above the Council in moral authority (though holding no formal
office) stand the **Lanemothers**: the senior pilots who hold the windlane
charts in memory. A Lanemother's refusal to fly a lane is, in practice, a
veto no Council vote can override.

The Moult recognizes no land law. Its one absolute statute is the **Tether
Rule**: whoever cuts a tether in anger answers to everyone the raft was
carrying.

### The Carrowspar Synod (the Ossuin holy spire)

Carrowspar is a lean, dark, south-hanging Spire two days windward of the
Crown, fissured top to bottom by the **Long Stair** — the carved descent-way
down which the Ossuin carry the dead of every kindred to the Undermist. It is
the seat of the **Deepway**, the Ossuin priesthood, and the only polity whose
authority every other polity acknowledges without a treaty: everybody dies,
and only the Deepway knows the way down.

Governed by the **Synod of Nine Silences** — nine senior priests who answer
questions in order of seniority, the most senior speaking least. Day-to-day
rule falls to the **Stairwardens**. Carrowspar mints no coin, fields no army,
and has never lost a negotiation that mattered.

Eleven years ago the Synod stopped accepting new diving commissions. They
have not explained. The backlog of un-descended dead, stored in wax and salt
in every spire's bone-cellars, is becoming a quiet, world-wide crisis.

### The Veyl Reefs (the Free Shoals)

A drowned-cluster of forty-odd small spires, bone-reefs, and tether-towns in
the world's warm east, where three windlanes braid together. No central
government; instead, the **Shoal Charters** — a web of mutual-defense and
toll-sharing compacts enforced by hired wings. The Reefs are where you go to
buy what the Crown bans, sell what the Moult won't carry, and hire wings who
don't ask questions. Pirate clutches (the **Lanhawks** worst among them)
winter in the unchartered outer reefs, and everyone pretends not to know
which tether-town fences their cargo.

### The Brightgyre Compact (the skyfarm league)

Where a permanent warm updraft (the **Gyre**) rises between four mid-sized
spires, the Alar have built the world's breadbasket: hundreds of acres of
netted skyweed terraces, krill-weirs, and rain-catch sails turning slowly in
the bright air. The four spires — Helsper, Aulia, Cantling, and Wrest — pool
sovereignty in the **Compact Table**, a council of farm-syndic delegates.
Brightgyre is rich, pacifist by policy, and utterly dependent on the Gyre
continuing to turn — which is why the Compact quietly funds the Gale Wardens
better than any other patron, and why its delegates wake sweating from dreams
of still air.

## Lesser Powers and Places

- **Lanternfall Spire** — a single-industry roost (lamp-crafting; its
  beeswax-and-marrowgas lanterns light half the world) of ~3,000, whose
  windlane is now dying. The campaign's opening crisis.
- **Greywake** — the drowned Spire, jutting from the Undermist at the heart
  of the oldest Stilling. Sovereign territory of nobody; congregation of the
  Hushed.
- **The Spindles** — a column of stripped, sinking spire husks abandoned over
  the centuries, now a vertical shantytown of the roostless. The Crown calls
  it a hazard; ten thousand people call it home.
- **Hollow Vesse** — a dead spire purchased outright by the Marrower Combine
  and converted into the world's only marrow refinery. Armed, private, and
  measurably lower in the sky every year.
- **The lane-shrines** — hundreds of small fixed roosts along every windlane:
  beacon-horn, rain-tank, three days' shelter, and a shrine-cantor who sings
  the lane-songs at dawn. Lane-shrines are neutral ground under the oldest
  law there is. Even Lanhawks honor it. Mostly.

## How Power Actually Works

1. **Lift is wealth.** A spire that loses marrow-breath sinks. Every polity's
   deepest books are lift-ledgers: how much breath the bone holds, how fast
   it seeps, what it costs to keep the nurseries on the high floors. The
   Marrower Combine's leverage is that it alone can move lift from where it
   is to where it's paid for — and its crime is where the lift comes from.
2. **Lanes are law.** Toll-rights on a windlane are inherited, married,
   gambled, and murdered for. The Moult's Lanemothers, the Crown's charters,
   and the Reefs' Shoal Charters are three incompatible legal theories of who
   owns the wind; most wars in history have been lane-wars.
3. **The Record is legitimacy.** The Quillate's archive arbitrates every
   treaty, debt, and lineage claim in the world. Whoever the Record favors,
   wins eventually. This is why the thousand-year hole at the bottom of the
   Record is not a scholarly curiosity. It is a loaded weapon nobody has
   fired yet.
4. **The Deepway is the constant.** Funeral right is the one service that
   cannot be substituted, boycotted, or counterfeited. The Synod's silence
   about why the diving stopped is therefore the loudest sound in world
   politics.

## Distances and Travel

Reckoned in **wind-days** (one day's flight for a laden courier riding a
lane, ~250 miles):

| Route | Wind-days | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Crown ↔ Carrowspar | 2 | the Mourner's Lane; always flyable, always cold |
| Crown ↔ Brightgyre | 3 | the Grain Lane; heaviest traffic in the world |
| Crown ↔ Veyl Reefs | 6–8 | three lane-changes; pirate risk past the Spindles |
| Crown ↔ Lanternfall | 4 | **lane dying — currently 9+ days by detour** |
| Crown ↔ Greywake | 5 | charted; nobody flies it; final lane-shrine abandoned |

Off-lane flying halves speed and doubles Fatigue; night flying is for
couriers, smugglers, and the desperate. Heavy cargo moves on marrow-gas
barges at a third of courier speed, which is why a sack of Brightgyre
krill-meal costs five times as much in the Reefs.
