# THE VEILWRACK — Alar Character Options
Careers, Windworking magic, racial abilities, movement, and gear.
Mechanics follow Mythras Imperative structures (cultures grant 100 skill
points, careers 100, bonus 150; or use the Skill Pyramid).

---

## Alar Racial Abilities (all kindreds)

- **Wings.** Flight (STR+DEX) is a Standard skill. Walk 4m, fly 12m.
- **Hollow bones.** SIZ −3 (min 4), DEX +2 at creation. Knockback distances
  against an Alar are doubled; falling damage is rolled normally but a Flight
  roll (Hard if a wing is wounded) halves effective fall distance — fully
  arrested on a critical.
- **Keen eyes.** Perception rolls relying on sight at long distance are one
  grade easier. Perception by smell is one grade harder.
- **Grounded dread.** An Alar who cannot fly (wing wound, Stilling, confined
  space) suffers Willpower rolls one grade harder against fear and panic
  effects. This is cultural as much as physical: flightlessness is the
  Veilwrack's image of death.
- **No swimmers.** Swim is a Professional skill for Alar; feathers waterlog
  in one round, after which Flight is impossible until dried (an hour by fire).

### Kindred bonuses
- **Vael:** +5 to one DEX-based skill; start with one extra lane-token
  contact (a named NPC along any windlane).
- **Roak:** +5 to one INT-based skill; literate by default (Literacy at base
  INT×2 free).
- **Ossuin:** +5 to Endurance *or* Willpower; may take **Mist-Rites**
  (see careers) without a teacher; +2 SIZ (partially offsetting the Alar −3).

## Windworking (Magic)

Windworking is the Mythras Imperative **Magic** system (SRD: Magic and
Powers), re-expressed: the caster coaxes the living Breath into small
intentional shapes. **Skill: Windworking (POW+CHA)** — a Professional skill
requiring a teacher; it fills the role of the SRD Magic skill exactly.

**Casting (SRD rules):** one Action + a Windworking roll.

| Roll result | Magic Points | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| Critical | 0 MP | spell works |
| Success | 1 MP | spell works |
| Failure | 1 MP | spell fails |
| Fumble | 1d3 MP | spell fails AND the Breath answers wrong (GM complication: a backdraft, a dead hush, a whisper to the wrong ear) |

- **Magic Points** = POW. Recovery: Healing Rate per hour of full rest —
  *house rule:* only in living wind. No MP recovers in still or bottled air.
- **Spell traits (SRD):** *Instant*, *Concentration*, *Ranged* (up to
  Windworking skill in metres), *Touch*, *Resist (Endurance/Evade/Willpower)*
  — resisted as an Opposed Roll vs the casting result; Evade resists cost an
  Action Point. Default duration: the scene. All spells Intensity/Magnitude 1.
- **Starting spells:** a character with the Windworking skill begins with
  **1d4+1 spells**. Learning a new spell costs **3 Experience Rolls plus a
  week of practice** with a teacher or a wind-script scroll.
- **Universal limits:** nothing works inside a Stilling. *The Slowing:* in
  Breath-degraded regions, casting is one Difficulty Grade harder.

### Powers (the SRD superpower framework) — GM-facing

Mythras Imperative's second system, **superpowers** (core powers + Boosts +
Limits, Power Points = POW), is reserved in the Veilwrack for beings touched
by the deep Breath. Use it, not the spell list, to build:

- **The Hushed** — powers framework with heavy Limits: *Life Support* (no
  breathing; immune to asphyxiation and exertion Fatigue), a custom *Silence
  Aura* core power (3m of dead air: Windworking impossible, hearing-Perception
  Formidable — and the aura slowly feeds the Stilling), with the Limits
  *Physical Limitation* (cannot fly) and *Limited Power* (all gifts fail in
  strong living wind).
- **The leviathan-touched** — a campaign-finale option: a character who
  survives intimate contact with an Unsung may gain ONE core power with one
  Limit (GM approval): *Flight* (boost-capable), *Elemental Control (air)*,
  *Enhanced Reactions*, or *Heightened Sense (air-pressure)*. This is the
  Breath beginning to answer a debt — flag it as a permanent, visible change
  (feathers gone white at the root, voice carrying further than it should).

## Movement & Aerial Rules

| Mode | Rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Walk | 4m | claw-feet: climbing bone-stone at full rate, no roll, if unencumbered |
| Fly (cruise) | 12m | sustainable for hours along a lane wind |
| Fly (run gait) | 36m | Athletics/Flight roll per minute or +1 Fatigue |
| Fly (sprint/stoop) | 60m | combat bursts and dives; one roll per round of sustained sprint |
| Hover | 0m | Flight roll each round; failure = +1 Fatigue. Hummingbird work is HARD for a person-sized wing. |
| Glide | 12m, −1m altitude per 10m | effortless; impossible in a Stilling |

- **Altitude bands** (GM shorthand): Deck (0–10m, melee possible with
  grounded foes), Low (10–100m), Lane (100m–1km, where the trade-gales run),
  High (above 1km: cold, thin — Endurance roll per hour), and the Roots
  (below the spires, turbulent — Flight rolls to maneuver).
- **Carrying aloft:** an Alar can fly carrying up to STR in 'Things';
  each Thing beyond that imposes one grade on Flight and −2m fly speed.
  Carrying another person requires double their SIZ in effective STR —
  in practice, two fliers and a sling.
- **Armor aloft:** flying with more than 12 total worn AP requires a Flight
  roll per scene; the highest worn AP still penalizes Initiative. Alar armor
  doctrine: torso and head only, wings bare.
- **Aerial engagement:** both fliers engaged in melee descend ~5m per round
  of fighting unless one spends an Action Point on Move to hold altitude.
  Trip becomes **Tumble** (1d10m altitude loss + Flight roll); Bash shoves
  in any of six directions.

## Equipment & Currency

**Currency:** the Crown mints **lacquer-scrip** (everyday: a meal is 1–2
scrip, a wingspear ~40); the Moult barters and keeps accounts in
**lane-favors**; high finance runs on **mist-pearls** (one pearl ≈ 500
scrip — and the Ossuin notice who's spending them).

**Weapons & armor:** see `rules/combat.md` sample tables (wingspear,
talon-hook, spurblade, stormbow, sling-darts, bola-net; windsilk 2 AP,
drake-leather 3 AP, bone-scale 4 AP, Wardens' alloy harness 5 AP).
