# ORGANIZATIONS OF THE VEILWRACK
### Patrons, cults, guilds, and orders — what they are, what they want, what they pay

*Each entry: (A) type & nature; (B) stated aims vs. actual aims; (C) ranks;
(D) member benefits; plus story hooks. Mythras cult/brotherhood mechanics
apply: rank advancement requires the listed skills, time in standing, and
the sponsorship of a superior. Organization skills marked ◆ are taught to
members one grade cheaper (2 Experience Rolls instead of 3 for new spells;
training time halved for skills).*

---

## 1. The Gale Wardens

**(A) Type & nature.** A chartered survey-and-rescue brotherhood, eleven
years old, ~400 wings worldwide in companies of 10–30. Part rangers, part
lifeboat service, part suppressed scientific society. The default PC patron.

**(B) What they say / what they want.** *Stated:* chart the Stillings, warn
the lanes, evacuate the threatened. *Actual:* exactly that — plus the thing
the charter forbids them to write down: find the **cause**. The Warden
command keeps a second map, the Acceleration Map, that would get them
dissolved for sedition if the Spirarchy saw it. Within the Wardens a quieter
faction (the "Causists," led in spirit by Commander Esk Veil-Torn) believes
the answer is historical, not meteorological, and steers good companies
toward old archives and older priests.

**(C) Ranks.** *Tail* (probationer) → *Wing* (full member, may fly rescues)
→ *Leadwing* (commands a flight of 3–5) → *Company-Commander* →
*Warden-Marshal* (one per region, three in the world). Advancement: Tail→Wing
needs Flight 60%+, Survival 50%+, one season's service; Wing→Leadwing needs
a documented rescue and First Aid or Navigation 60%+.

**(D) Benefits.** Roost and rations at any Warden post; lane-toll exemption
(charter right, grudgingly honored); ◆ Survival, Navigation, First Aid;
access to still-gauges and breath-bottles at cost; the *moral* benefit that
strangers feed you and innkeepers wake you gently. Also: standing legal
peril, since half your field reports are technically sedition.

**Hooks.** Every Stilling is a job. Evacuations under time pressure; a
lane-shrine that stopped answering its beacon-horn; a company that went into
the Greywake fringe nine strong and radioed back in *writing*; the order from
above to hand the Acceleration Map to a Crown Agent — comply, refuse, or
copy it first?

## 2. The Spirarchy of Suruveil's Crown (and the Crown Agents)

**(A)** The state church-government of the Crown Dominion: a sprawling civil
service of Gallery Lords, ring-magistrates, lane-charter clerks, and festival
priests, crowned by the Spirarch. Its inner service, the **Crown Agents**
(~80 operatives), answers to the Spirarch alone.

**(B) Stated:** order, continuity, the Settling Right; the Stillings are
natural weather and panic is the real plague. **Actual:** the Spirarchy's
core knows the Tenth Gallery holds the Founding Compact and that legitimacy
itself dies if the founding is ever un-sealed. Velute the Ninth is not
hiding a crime for power's sake; she inherited a lie load-bearing enough
that removing it might drop the political sky on everyone. The Agents'
actual mission: manage who learns what, and *measure* the Stillings secretly
while denying them publicly.

**(C) Ranks.** Civil: *Petitioner* → *Sworn Clerk* → *Ring-Warden* →
*Gallery Officer* → *Gallery Lord*. Agents: *Listener* (informant) →
*Plume* (field agent) → *Quiet Plume* (licensed for anything) → *the
Spirarch's Shadow* (one; identity unknown even to Gallery Lords).

**(D) Benefits.** Salary in lacquer-scrip, gallery housing one ring above
your birth, legal immunity short of treason; Agents get ◆ Influence,
Deceit, Insight, false charters, and the best still-gauges in the world
(the Agents' private data is better than the Wardens'). The price is that
the organization owns your testimony forever.

**Hooks.** PCs need a sealed chart; an Agent offers it — for a name. A
sympathetic Ring-Warden feeds the Wardens suppressed data and is about to be
caught. The Spirarch summons the company personally: she wants the truth
found *and* contained, and is honest about both.

## 3. The Quillate

**(A)** The Roak archive-guild: scriptorium, university, court of record,
and weather bureau in one, occupying the Crown's upper galleries with
chapter-houses on twenty spires. ~3,000 sworn archivists.

**(B) Stated:** completeness of the Record, neutrality in all disputes,
knowledge as the common lift of the world. **Actual:** completeness *except
one hole*. The Quillate's deep leadership knows the millennium gap was cut
deliberately by the first Recension and that the institution's neutrality is
founded on its complicity. High Quill Orrocan sealed the deepest archive in
999 not to hide the hole but because someone had begun *filling it in* —
documents he could not source appearing in the stacks, in a hand no living
scribe writes. He has told no one.

**(C) Ranks.** *Inkbond* (apprentice) → *Quill* (journeyman; may witness
contracts) → *Gallery Quill* (stack access by floor) → *Recensor* (may
correct the Record; 40 in the world) → *High Quill*. Advancement by
examination: Literacy and Lore specialties at 70%+ for Gallery Quill;
Recensor requires a lifetime and three Recensors' sponsorship.

**(D) Benefits.** Stack access (graded), ◆ Lore (any), ◆ Literacy, ◆
Commerce; a Quill's witness-mark makes contracts enforceable world-wide;
chapter-house lodging; the subtle armor of being the person every faction
needs alive to certify its claims.

**Hooks.** Archive heist (the classic — the PCs as thieves or guards); a
Recensor offers to trade deep-stack access for an "errand" in the Spindles;
the forged-or-not documents in the sealed stacks describe *the hunt of a
great beast*, and Orrocan needs deniable outsiders to verify one checkable
detail at Greywake.

## 4. The Deepway (the Ossuin priesthood)

**(A)** The funeral order of the world: priests, stairwardens, chant-keepers,
and (until 996) death-divers, seated at Carrowspar under the Synod of Nine
Silences. The only organization with members of all kindreds in its lay
ranks and only Ossuin in its ordained ones.

**(B) Stated:** carry the dead down; keep the door; maintain the chant.
**Actual:** the same — the Deepway is the rare institution whose public
creed is its real one — but with a ledger behind it: the priesthood has
always known the Breath is a *debt*, and the moratorium exists because the
last diver, Sister Ulvane, returned in 996 reporting that the Unsung below
have begun hatching hungry into water with nothing left to feed them. The
Synod is not concealing a crime. It is concealing a *deadline*.

**(C) Ranks.** *Lay-bearer* (any kindred; carries biers to the
descent-stations) → *Sworn of the Stair* → *Chant-Keeper* → *Stairwarden* →
*Diver* (ordained; currently none active) → *Synod*. Endurance 60%+ and
Willpower 70%+ to be Sworn; the chant (a Lore specialty) at 90% for
Chant-Keeper, learned only orally.

**(D) Benefits.** Universal safe-conduct (harming a bier-party is the one
crime every polity punishes identically); ◆ Willpower, ◆ Endurance, ◆ Lore
(Funeral Rite); breath-bottle and cold-wax training; mist-pearl stipend for
the ordained. Members are also the only people lawfully permitted below the
root-line — a license worth more than money to certain employers.

**Hooks.** A lay-bearer PC is asked to carry *up* something a descent-party
found lodged in a bier-hook (a harpoon barb of worked metal, very old). The
un-buried dead crisis turns a roost violent. Sister Ulvane has left
Carrowspar without leave, looking for "wings who can keep silence," and the
Synod will pay anyone who returns her — while she will pay the same wings to
hear her out first.

## 5. The Lane-Shrine Cantorate

**(A)** The distributed religion of the windlanes: several hundred
shrine-cantors, one to three per shrine, who keep the beacon-horns, the
rain-tanks, and the **lane-songs** — the sung schedules by which the lanes
are flown. Less a hierarchy than a guild of hermits with perfect pitch.

**(B) Stated:** sing the dawn, shelter the traveler, keep the lane. **
Actual:** the same, plus one thing the Cantorate has never told anyone
because nobody asked: the lane-songs are *tuned to the lanes*, and cantors
adjust them as the winds change. Their accumulated song-corrections, hummed
shrine to shrine for nine centuries, are the finest record of the Breath's
decline in existence — encoded in a medium no archive can subpoena.

**(C) Ranks.** *Wick* (shrine-servant) → *Cantor* (holds one shrine) →
*Lane-Cantor* (holds a whole lane's song; ~40) → *the Dawn Chorus* (the
informal council of Lane-Cantors, which has met four times in history). A
Wick becomes a Cantor by singing the lane-song flawlessly through a full
storm-night, alone.

**(D) Benefits.** A shrine (roost, tank, stores, horn); the absolute
neutrality of shrine-ground; ◆ Sing, ◆ Insight, ◆ Lore (Windlanes);
travelers' tithes; and the love of everyone who flies — a cantor in trouble
finds the sky suddenly full of couriers.

**Hooks.** The cantor of the dying Lanternfall lane refuses evacuation; her
shrine's song, sung continuously, is visibly slowing the Stilling's edge
(the cult's secret, independently discovered). The Dawn Chorus convenes for
the fifth time in history and needs trustworthy escort wings. Someone is
murdering cantors along the Grain Lane and replacing their songs with
*almost* identical ones.

## 6. The Marrower Combine

**(A)** The lift cartel: tapping crews, refinery (Hollow Vesse), barge
fleets, lamp-gas franchises. Part guild, part company, part organized crime,
depending on the spire. ~6,000 employees; run by a board of Gas Barons, of
whom **Brell Coinfeather** is the most public.

**(B) Stated:** light the world, lift the world; tapping is harmless ("the
bone re-breathes, friends — it's a *renewable*"). **Actual:** the board has
run the numbers twice; tapped spires sink measurably faster; both studies
were burned and their authors paid off or worse. The Combine's real project
is to convert lift-wealth into political safety *before* the truth surfaces
— buying charters, magistrates, and respectability. Brell, alone among the
Barons, keeps a private copy of the burned numbers, as insurance or as a
conscience he hasn't decided whether to have.

**(C) Ranks.** *Tapper* → *Crew Boss* → *Vesse Agent* (franchise/contract
authority) → *Baron's Hand* → *Gas Baron* (board seat, bought in marrow
futures).

**(D) Benefits.** The best wages in the world for dangerous work; company
scrip good at any franchise lamp-house; ◆ Mechanisms, ◆ Athletics
(root-work), ◆ Commerce; for Hands and up: a private barge network that
moves anything, no questions. The Combine also genuinely takes care of its
maimed — funded by exactly the thing maiming them.

**Hooks.** A tapping crew has broken through into a marrow-chamber that is
*still warm*, and the Crew Boss wants outside experts before he wants his
Baron. Brell offers the PCs the burned numbers — price: extraction of a
witness the Combine wants silenced, alive, *out* of another Baron's reach.
Hollow Vesse's sink-rate just crossed the line the refinery's own engineers
call unrecoverable, and ten thousand workers don't know.

## 7. The Hushed Choir

**(A)** A mendicant cult, perhaps 300 living members plus an unknown number
of Hushed, who venerate the Stilling as the world's overdue rest — "the
world breathing in." Founded twenty years ago (before Greywake, note) by the
poet Cassan Veil, who walked into the first Stilling in 994 and is claimed
by the cult to walk there yet.

**(B) Stated:** the Stilling is holy; the Hushed are saints, not victims;
the Choir eases the world's passage by singing at the edges of dead air.
**Actual:** layered. The outer cult believes the creed. The inner circle
(the **Sotto Voce**, led by Cantor Hssh) knows two enormous practical
truths: how to enter and leave a Stilling alive, and that sustained
Windworking *pushes the edge back* — knowledge they guard precisely because
the world that suppressed still-panic would weaponize it without
understanding the price. What even the Sotto Voce does not know: what the
Stillings are. Their theology is sincere, wrong, and closer than anyone
else's.

**(C) Ranks.** *Listener* (sympathizer) → *Singer* (sworn; has stood at an
edge and sung) → *Edge-Walker* (has entered dead air and returned; ~30) →
*Sotto Voce* (inner circle; ~7) → *the Quieted* (the Hushed themselves,
ambiguously revered as the highest rank or mourned as the cost).

**(D) Benefits.** The cult will teach Edge-Walkers what no one else can:
Stilling survival craft (sign-speech, still-walking, the entry disciplines
— mechanically: ◆ Willpower, the *Edge-Craft* Lore, and the GM's mercy on
Quieting rolls for the well-trained). They feed the poor at every fringe.
The price is being a member of the most universally hated congregation in
the sky.

**Hooks.** The Choir requests Warden escort for a "pilgrimage" that is
plainly also a resupply run to the Hushed at Greywake — refusing means the
supplies don't arrive and the Hushed come *out*. Cantor Hssh writes to a PC
by name. A Singer defects, bringing the entry disciplines to the open
market, and every faction in the world converges on one frightened cultist.

## 8. The Featherwrights' Benevolent Guild

**(A)** The artisan league: glider-wrights, rope-walkers, bone-joiners,
lamp-makers, physicians, and (its quiet aristocracy) the **wing-surgeons**.
Chapter-houses on every settled spire; the closest thing the roostless and
gallery-poor have to an institution of their own.

**(B) Stated:** fair work, sound craft, care for broken wings; the guild
oath ends *"no one falls whom a making could have held."* **Actual:** that,
sincerely — plus a slow-burning political project: the guild keeps the
world's only honest actuarial tables (falls, sinkings, wing-wounds,
lamp-failures), and its leadership has noticed what the tables imply about
the Combine, the Spirarchy's denials, and the future. The guild is deciding,
chapter by chapter, what to do about being right.

**(C) Ranks.** *Apprentice* → *Journeyman* → *Master* (by masterwork
examination) → *Chapter Warden* → *Grand Warden of the Bench*. Wing-surgeons
rank as Masters with their own examinations (Healing 80%+).

**(D) Benefits.** Trade training at guild rates (◆ Craft of choice, ◆
Mechanisms or ◆ Healing); tool-right (guild workshops anywhere); the
**Fallen Fund** — the guild pays for members' wing-surgery, full stop; and
guild arbitration, which even the Crown's courts respect. For PCs: the guild
is where you repair gliders, set wings, commission gear, and hear what the
actuarial tables are whispering.

**Hooks.** A wing-surgeon needs Stillwight gland-glass — found only in dead
air — to attempt a first-of-its-kind surgery on a Hushed child's mother. The
Grand Warden quietly commissions an independent verification of the
Combine's sink data. A masterwork glider is stolen before its examination,
and the apprentice accused is the only member who *couldn't* have built it.

## Using Organizations in Play

- **Every PC should start with one membership** (rank 1–2) chosen at
  creation; it supplies training discounts, a patron, and a problem.
- **Rank advancement is story.** Promotions come from play — a documented
  rescue, a sung storm-night, a masterwork — never from bookkeeping alone.
- **Benefits have handles.** Everything an organization gives is something
  it can ask back. The membership that feeds you in Act One is the
  membership that orders you to hand over the map in Act Three.
- **Cross-loyalties are the engine.** The ideal party holds four different
  memberships and one shared mission; the campaign's decision points are
  built to split exactly those loyalties.
