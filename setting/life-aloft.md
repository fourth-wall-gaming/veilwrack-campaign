# LIFE ALOFT
### Daily life, calendar, law, language, and custom in the Veilwrack

*The connective tissue: everything a character simply knows from having
grown up in the sky.*

---

## The Calendar and the Festivals

The year is told by winds, not months: four seasons, each ~90 days, counted
in **nines** (the nine-day week, one day per Synod silence, say the Ossuin;
one per gallery, says the Crown; because nine divides ninety, say the Roak).

- **The High Gales** (the year's start — now): cold, fast, brilliant air;
  courier season; the long-haul trading runs. Festival: **First Lift**, when
  every fledgling who learned to fly that year flies the circuit of their
  home spire while the whole roost drums.
- **The Wet**: the Shade's clouds climb; rain-catch season; the skyfarm
  harvest. Festival: **Tanksfull**, communal, soaked, and the traditional
  season for weddings ("marry when the water's in").
- **The Long Calm**: hot, slow air; barge season; repairs, festivals,
  scholarship. Festival: **the Patching** — gliders, rooves, and quarrels
  are all to be mended before the Calm ends. Public reconciliation is a
  civic duty; carrying a grudge across the Patching is genuinely shocking.
- **The Mourning Fogs**: the Undermist breathes up; visibility dies; flying
  is grim. The season of funerals — the year's dead, wax-kept, are carried
  down in convoy on the **Stairfall** days. Festival, if it is one:
  **Lastlight**, when every spire burns one lantern per death at the
  root-line, and the Undermist glows back.

Years are counted After the Settling (A.S.); the current year is 1001.
"Millennium dread" is a real cultural force — the round number and the
Stillings arrived together, and everyone noticed.

## Names

- **Vael:** short given name + deed-name or lane-name, earned and changeable:
  *Sefa Half-Light, Kithrel of the Moult, Esk Veil-Torn.* Asking how a Vael
  got their second name is polite; assuming, rude.
- **Roak:** given name + lineage name from the mother's archive-line:
  *Orrocan, Velute, Pellin Graveswright.* Roak introduce themselves with
  profession: "Velute, Recensor's line, weather."
- **Ossuin:** one name only, given at the first descent: *Ulvane, Hssh.*
  Family is replaced by stair-cohort; an Ossuin's "siblings" descended the
  same year.
- **Spindles/roostless:** given name + whatever sticks: *Ammet Threadbare,
  Brell Coinfeather.* A Spindles name with three parts means someone is
  putting on airs or hiding.

## Law and Justice

Three legal cultures, one shared bedrock:

- **The shared bedrock — Sky Law**, older than the Compact, enforced
  everywhere by everyone: (1) shrine-ground is inviolate; (2) a bier-party
  passes unharmed; (3) no one cuts a tether, fouls a rain-tank, or taps
  another roost's marrow — these three are *fall-crimes*, punished by the
  Long Drop (cast down, wings bound) in every jurisdiction. Even the
  Lanhawks flog rather than shelter a tether-cutter.
- **Crown law:** written, codified, gallery-graded (a Ninth-Gallery oath
  weighs less than a Second's in court — reformers hate this; it persists).
  Punishments run to fines, gallery demotion, archive erasure (civil death),
  and for sedition the Quiet Cells, which are exactly what they sound like.
- **Moult law:** restitution, not punishment. Every wrong is priced in
  lane-favors by the raft-council; what cannot be repaid is worked off. The
  unpayable wrong is exile — "given to the open sky" — which the Vael
  consider a death sentence with extra steps and the Crown keeps mistaking
  for leniency.
- **Reef law:** the contract is god; the arbiter is whoever both parties'
  charters name; the appeal is weather.

## Money and Daily Economy

- **Lacquer-scrip** (Crown): bone chits sealed in numbered lacquer; good
  wherever the Crown's credit is. Daily wage, dock-hand: ~2 scrip. A good
  glider: 300. A steel knife: a year's wages.
- **Lane-favors** (Moult): a ledgered debt of carriage — "one parcel, one
  passenger, one season's priority." The Vael economy is favors all the way
  down; scrip is for strangers.
- **Mist-pearls** (everywhere, uneasily): the high-value store. Pearls
  don't inflate, don't rot, and are only made by the Deepway, which prices
  them in services rendered to the dead. Carrying many marks you as rich,
  pious, or a robber of one of the two.
- Most folk eat skyweed cakes, krill-paste, eggs, and roost-fowl; drink
  rain, weed-beer, and (if Vael) fermented krill-brine that outsiders
  describe with their whole face. Brightgyre grain is wealth; real fruit is
  legend and festival.

## Living on a Spire

A settled spire is a vertical city: crown (watch-roosts, beacons, the
wealthy's wind-gardens), galleries (carved ring-streets, workshops,
markets), waist (the great middle warren of homes), root-line (docks for
barges, Marrower works, the descent-stations), all stitched by flight-wells
— vertical shafts with right-of-way rules fledglings learn before grammar
("rise on the sunward side; sink on the shade").

Homes are **roosts**: bone-carved rooms with perch-lofts, woven screens, and
always — law and custom both — two exits. Doors are for weather, not
privacy; privacy is made with screens and with the elaborate Alar etiquette
of *not having heard things*. Fledglings sleep in clutch-lofts; elders keep
the lowest, warmest rooms, closest to the level they'll leave by.

## Etiquette Worth Knowing

- Greet by showing both palms then folding the wings full shut: "I am not
  landing on you; I am staying." Wings half-open during conversation
  signals hurry or distrust.
- Gifts of *lift* (a flask of marrowgas, a wing-mend, paid passage)
  outrank gifts of goods.
- Never touch another's primaries. Wing-grooming is for kin, lovers, and
  surgeons.
- Whistling indoors is rude (it mimics shear-wind); singing is always
  permitted; a roost that bans singing is hiding something, ask any Vael.
- The dead are spoken of in the present tense until they have been carried
  down, then in the past. Getting this wrong is the fastest way to clear a
  room.
- "May your air move" is the universal parting. Its inversion — *still air
  find you* — is the worst thing one Alar can say to another, and in the
  last decade it has stopped being said at all, even in anger.

## Rumors Current in 1001 A.S. (table seed)

1. The Lanternfall lane sings flat — the shrine-cantor has rewritten the
   dawn-song twice this season. (True; see organizations.md.)
2. The Synod has begun *buying back* mist-pearls. Quietly. Above market.
   (True; ask why.)
3. A Quillate deep-stack burned last Fogs — except nothing's missing.
   (False — worse: something's been *added*.)
4. Brell Coinfeather has commissioned a glider built for two with sealed
   cargo-space and no colors. (True.)
5. The Hushed at Greywake have started building. Nobody agrees what.
   (True. Act 3.)
6. There's a ninth Stilling, small, perfectly round, directly above the
   Crown — too high to reach, only visible at dusk. (False. For now. Use
   the dread.)
