## The Threat: The Stilling

Eleven years ago, a Vael courier flying the Greywake windlane hit air that
did not move. Not calm air — *dead* air: thick, soundless, syrup-slow, where
wings find no purchase and lanterns burn low and blue. She barely glided
clear. Within a season the dead zone had swallowed the whole windlane. Within
five years it had reached Greywake Spire itself — and Greywake, its marrow
breath gone quiet, **sank**, screaming and crumbling, into the Undermist,
taking nine thousand Alar with it.

The zones are called **Stillings**. There are now at least seven, and they
are growing. Inside a Stilling:

- **Flight fails.** Each round of attempted flight in still air requires a
  Flight roll (Hard); failure accrues a level of Fatigue. Gliding is
  impossible; there are no thermals. Most who enter walk out along
  spire-bridges — or do not come out.
- **Sound dies.** Voices carry no more than arm's length. All Perception
  by hearing is Herculean. Alar instinctively hate this more than anything.
- **The Breath is absent.** Windworking (the Alar magic of shaped air) does
  not function at all.
- **Things live there now.** The Stillwights — drifting, glassy predators
  that drink the moving air out of living lungs. And worse: the **Hushed** —
  Alar who were caught in a Stilling and came back changed. Silent. Wrong.
  Breathing *out* quiet the way a fire breathes out smoke.

No one knows what causes a Stilling, how to predict one, or how to stop one.
The Spirarchy of Suruveil's Crown insists the zones are natural weather and
punishes "still-panic" as sedition. The Vael have begun rerouting the
windlanes. The Ossuin have stopped accepting new diving commissions and will
not say why. And the Quillate's deepest archive has been sealed by order of
its own High Quill.
