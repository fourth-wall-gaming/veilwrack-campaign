## 8. The Featherwrights' Benevolent Guild

**(A)** The artisan league: glider-wrights, rope-walkers, bone-joiners,
lamp-makers, physicians, and (its quiet aristocracy) the **wing-surgeons**.
Chapter-houses on every settled spire; the closest thing the roostless and
gallery-poor have to an institution of their own.

**(B) Stated:** fair work, sound craft, care for broken wings; the guild
oath ends *"no one falls whom a making could have held."* **Actual:** that,
sincerely — plus a slow-burning political project: the guild keeps the
world's only honest actuarial tables (falls, sinkings, wing-wounds,
lamp-failures), and its leadership has noticed what the tables imply about
the Combine, the Spirarchy's denials, and the future. The guild is deciding,
chapter by chapter, what to do about being right.

**(C) Ranks.** *Apprentice* → *Journeyman* → *Master* (by masterwork
examination) → *Chapter Warden* → *Grand Warden of the Bench*. Wing-surgeons
rank as Masters with their own examinations (Healing 80%+).

**(D) Benefits.** Trade training at guild rates (◆ Craft of choice, ◆
Mechanisms or ◆ Healing); tool-right (guild workshops anywhere); the
**Fallen Fund** — the guild pays for members' wing-surgery, full stop; and
guild arbitration, which even the Crown's courts respect. For PCs: the guild
is where you repair gliders, set wings, commission gear, and hear what the
actuarial tables are whispering.

**Hooks.** A wing-surgeon needs Stillwight gland-glass — found only in dead
air — to attempt a first-of-its-kind surgery on a Hushed child's mother. The
Grand Warden quietly commissions an independent verification of the
Combine's sink data. A masterwork glider is stolen before its examination,
and the apprentice accused is the only member who *couldn't* have built it.
