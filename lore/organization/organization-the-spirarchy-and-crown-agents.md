## 2. The Spirarchy of Suruveil's Crown (and the Crown Agents)

**(A)** The state church-government of the Crown Dominion: a sprawling civil
service of Gallery Lords, ring-magistrates, lane-charter clerks, and festival
priests, crowned by the Spirarch. Its inner service, the **Crown Agents**
(~80 operatives), answers to the Spirarch alone.

**(B) Stated:** order, continuity, the Settling Right; the Stillings are
natural weather and panic is the real plague. **Actual:** the Spirarchy's
core knows the Tenth Gallery holds the Founding Compact and that legitimacy
itself dies if the founding is ever un-sealed. Velute the Ninth is not
hiding a crime for power's sake; she inherited a lie load-bearing enough
that removing it might drop the political sky on everyone. The Agents'
actual mission: manage who learns what, and *measure* the Stillings secretly
while denying them publicly.

**(C) Ranks.** Civil: *Petitioner* → *Sworn Clerk* → *Ring-Warden* →
*Gallery Officer* → *Gallery Lord*. Agents: *Listener* (informant) →
*Plume* (field agent) → *Quiet Plume* (licensed for anything) → *the
Spirarch's Shadow* (one; identity unknown even to Gallery Lords).

**(D) Benefits.** Salary in lacquer-scrip, gallery housing one ring above
your birth, legal immunity short of treason; Agents get ◆ Influence,
Deceit, Insight, false charters, and the best still-gauges in the world
(the Agents' private data is better than the Wardens'). The price is that
the organization owns your testimony forever.

**Hooks.** PCs need a sealed chart; an Agent offers it — for a name. A
sympathetic Ring-Warden feeds the Wardens suppressed data and is about to be
caught. The Spirarch summons the company personally: she wants the truth
found *and* contained, and is honest about both.
