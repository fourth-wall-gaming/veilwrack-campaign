## GM Appendix: closing the loop (GM only)

The Vital Circulation Hypothesis is **correct**, and the campaign's secret
is its missing final chapter: the Anemoi dwindled naturally over ages, but
the *last* one — Suruveil, the Worldlung, the single remaining organ
sustaining the whole circulation — was killed by the Alar founders a
thousand years ago (see gm-secrets.md). Everything since is inventory
rundown:

- The Breath's smooth decline curve = depletion of the reservoir Suruveil
  left (marrow-breath in every husk, the "fat inheritance").
- Stillings = the reservoir's local zero-points, appearing first where
  circulation was always thinnest (the model predicts the *next* zones —
  this is the Acceleration Map's real content, and the Wardens' analysts
  are within two years of deriving it).
- The Hushed = the same depletion at body scale: the Quieting is the dead
  zone equilibrating with the one pocket of living air a trapped Alar
  carries — their own. What remains is a person-shaped low-pressure system
  in the Breath: animate, conscious, and *contagiously still*.
- Windworking pushing back Stilling edges = re-seeding dead air with living
  air at the worker's own metabolic cost. Scaled up (a city's lungs, a
  bottled spire, a willing company), the same mechanism wakes an Unsung:
  leviathan eggs are dormant for lack of exactly this trigger-stock.
- The Undermist's night-glow: Anemoi larval microfauna — the bottom rung of
  the leviathan ecology, still alive down there, still waiting for the
  ladder above them to be rebuilt. The Ossuin night-songs call the glow
  "the nursery lights." The Ossuin, as usual, are not being metaphorical.

**Plausibility stance for rulings:** allow anything consistent with "real
planet + one biological miracle (the living-air ecology)." When players
probe physics, reward them — the world is *meant* to be solvable, and every
correct inference (buoyancy, depletion curves, the lamp-burn series) is a
step toward the truth the institutions buried.
