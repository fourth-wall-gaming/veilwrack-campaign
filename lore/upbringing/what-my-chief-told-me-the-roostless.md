## What My Chief Told Me (a Spindles childhood, the roostless)

Chief Ammet Threadbare told me this on the gleaning nets under Spindle
Seven, because that is where the children of nobody get their answers.

**Where the world came from.** Who knows, cub? The Vael say a herd, the
Roak say a book, the Ossuin say a breath, and every one of those stories
ends with *their* kindred holding something and ours holding the net. Here
is what your chief knows for certain: the world came from somewhere, and by
the time it got to us it was *secondhand*. We live in the spires nobody
wanted, fish the air nobody charted, burn the lamp-dregs. Whatever made the
world, it had heirs, and then it had us.

**Why we die.** Cold, mostly. Falls. Bad air in a stripped husk. The pretty
answers are for people who die in beds. But I'll give you the Spindles
answer too, the real one: we die *unrecorded*, and that is the part to be
angry about. The Quillate writes down every Crown merchant's sneeze; when
Spindle Nine shed its skin-quarter in the storm of '88, the Record gave it
one line. So we keep our own book — the Thread-book, every name, every
fall, sewn in knots because paper rots. You'll learn the knots. That's your
letters, cub. Nobody is forgotten off *my* nets.

**Why we are here.** To catch what falls. That's the work and the creed
both. The high galleries drop things — cargo, chances, people — and the
Spindles catch them, mend them, and sell them back. You are here because
somebody's grandmother caught somebody's grandfather in a net, and you'll
stay alive by being the kind of person others would dive after. Loyalty's
the only lift we own. Hold your people the way bone holds breath.

**The others.** The Crown is weather: real, dangerous, not personal —
don't curse at it, just rig for it. The Moult will feed you and never let
you forget the feeding; fly with them a season, learn everything, come
home. The Quillate is a locked door with the truth behind it — every
Spindles cub dreams of robbing it; the smart ones grow up to get *hired*
into it, which is the better robbery. The Deepway is the one debt we pay
first — they carried our dead down free in the year of the storm and wrote
nothing in any ledger about it, and the Spindles do not forget that the way
the Record forgets us. The Marrowers will offer you wages, cub, good ones,
and some year when the nets are thin you'll take them — just count what
they pay you against what they're draining out from under everyone, and
come home before the arithmetic makes you one of them. And the Hushed?
We're the only folk who've fed them, you know. Left bread on the dead-line
at Greywake fringe. It was gone in the morning, and *something* had
arranged the crumbs in a circle. Make of that what you like. Your chief
makes of it: even the emptied are somebody's people, and nobody else was
leaving bread.
