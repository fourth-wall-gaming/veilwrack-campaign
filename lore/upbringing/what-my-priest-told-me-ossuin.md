## What My Priest Told Me (an Ossuin childhood, Carrowspar)

The Stairwarden told me this on the Long Stair itself, on my seventh
descent, where the lamplight goes red and you can hear the Undermist turn
over below you like a sleeper.

**Where the world came from.** Breathe out. Now wait — feel that need, the
little death before you breathe in? Hold the feeling. That is the oldest
fact. The world is a breath that something breathed *out*, fledgling, and
all of us — wing, bone, lamp, song — live inside that exhalation. The great
ones breathed the world; the world is the shape of their generosity. We
nest in their bones. We light our lamps with their marrow. Say it plainly,
because our whole kindred's office is to say plain things slowly: *we live
inside the dead, on the goodness of the dead, and we have not said thank
you properly in a thousand years.*

**Why we die.** To pay. Death is not punishment and not accident; it is the
installment. Every living thing borrows breath at birth and returns it at
death, and the place of returning is below, where the mist keeps the
world's accounts. This is why we carry the dead down — every kindred's dead,
the beggar and the Spirarch at the same shoulder-height — and why the chant
says what it says. You will sing the line ten thousand times in your life:
*we return what was taken; we do not return enough.* When you are older you
will notice the line does not say *what was given*. Notice it quietly.

**Why we are here.** To keep the door. Between the living sky and the deep
that renews it there must be a kindred that goes down with clean hands and
comes back with shut mouths, or the door becomes a market — imagine
Marrowers in the Undermist; you will need no other image of sin. We are
heavy, slow, long-lived, hard to frighten: built for the office. Mind the
door, mind the debt, mind the dead. Everything else — the pearls, the
politics, the Synod's nine chairs — is scaffolding around those three
duties.

**The others.** Love them; they are why the door matters. The Vael, who pay
their dead down promptly and grieve in motion — when a Moult raft drops
tether and circles Carrowspar in silence, that is mourning done correctly.
The Roak, who want to *copy the chant* — refuse them gently, forever; some
documents are true only in a living mouth. The Crown, which believes it
rules us because we have never corrected it. And the Hushed. Fledgling, the
others fear the Hushed wrongly. They are not the debt collected. They are
the debt *defaulted* — what remains when the world can no longer pay its
side. The Synod stopped the diving because of what the last diver heard
below. I will not tell you what she heard. I will tell you the Synod did
not stop out of fear. We stopped the way a family stops spending when it
finally reads the ledger.
