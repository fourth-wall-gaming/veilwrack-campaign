## The World

The Veilwrack is an ocean of open sky. Far below everything lies the
**Undermist**: a luminous, slow-boiling cloud-sea, faintly gold by day and
green-lit from within by night. Nothing that descends into the Undermist
returns, save the Ossuin death-divers — and they only skim its surface, and
they do not speak of what they hear down there.

There is no land. Civilization clings to the **Spires** — colossal hollow
towers of pale, porous bone-stone, some a half-mile tall, that float at
altitude like anchored islands. The learned know what the common roost-folk
prefer to forget: the Spires are not stone. They are the calcified husks of
the **Anemoi**, the sky-leviathans of the elder world — vast coiling beings,
part whale and part weather-front, whose marrow still holds *breath*: living
wind, trapped in the bone, which keeps each husk aloft.

The wind of the Veilwrack is not mere moving air. It is the **Breath** — a
living medium. Thermals behave with intent; trade-gales run on schedules the
Vael have charted for forty generations; a still day is an omen. Every Alar
culture has its own name for the Breath and its own way of listening to it.
