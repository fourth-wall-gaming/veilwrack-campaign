# A HISTORY OF THE VEILWRACK
### As the Record tells it — and where the Record goes quiet

*All dates are reckoned **A.S.** — After the Settling, the founding of
Suruveil's Crown. The current year is 1001 A.S.*

---

## The Elder Sky (before the Record)

Of the world before the Settling, the Record holds almost nothing, and what
the kindreds hold instead is song.

The songs agree on this much: the elder sky was **crowded with giants**. The
Anemoi — sky-leviathans vast as weather — coiled through the high air, and
the winds of the world ran warm and strong in their wake. The Alar of that
age were roostless: small clutch-bands following the leviathans the way gulls
follow a ship, gleaning, riding their pressure-wakes, nesting in the drifting
husks of the giant dead. The Vael songs call it the Long Following and make
it sound like freedom. The Ossuin chants call it the Debt-Making and make it
sound like something else.

Why the giants dwindled, the songs do not agree — plague, age, a sky grown
too cold, a sin. By the last century of the elder age there were few, and
then there was one, and the songs of all three kindreds go carefully,
identically silent on what happened next.

## The Hole (c. −60 to 0 A.S.)

Here is the fact that respectable scholarship walks around: **the Record has
a hole in it.** The Quillate's archive, the proudest unbroken chronicle in
the world, *begins* in Year 0 with a fully-formed founding charter in three
mature scripts — and holds nothing before it. No drafts. No genealogies
running backward. Sixty-odd years of the late elder age, the lifetimes of the
very founders, are simply absent, and the edges of the absence are clean.
Documents that should reference those years have been re-copied with the
references gone; the re-copying itself dates to the first century A.S.

The official Quillate position is that the pre-Settling Alar were not yet
literate and there is nothing to miss. The official position is taught,
examined, and quietly disbelieved by every archivist good enough to be
trusted with the deep stacks.

## The Settling (Year 0)

In Year 0, the three kindreds together settled the greatest husk in the sky
— the colossal horned Spire now called Suruveil's Crown — and signed the
**Founding Compact**, the treaty that apportioned the world: lanes to the
Vael, the Record and the workshops to the Roak, the funeral right and the
deep places to the Ossuin. The Compact's original text is held in the
Spirarchy's sealed Tenth Gallery and has not been publicly read in eight
centuries; its terms are known only through the digest in the Record.

Every polity in the world traces its legitimacy to this single document
nobody may read.

## The Compact Era (1–290 A.S.)

Three centuries of settlement. The kindreds spread along the great lanes,
charting and naming, raising roosts on every sound husk. The lane-shrine
network was sung into place rope by rope. Carrowspar's Long Stair was carved
(a labor of ninety years). The population of the world perhaps tripled.

It was also the era that built the world's quiet machinery of forgetting:
the Quillate's first Grand Recension (in which "corrupted" early documents
were re-copied and the originals burned), the Spirarchy's codification of the
Settling Right, and the Deepway's closure of the Undermist to all but its own
divers — three institutions, three locks, one door.

## The Charter Wars (290–355 A.S.)

The first great breakdown. As the good husks filled, younger lineages
demanded lanes of their own; the Vael Lanemothers refused to re-divide what
the Compact had apportioned. Sixty years of raids, tether-cuttings, and
lane-blockades followed — the only full war the Veilwrack has ever fought,
and it killed lift as much as people: four spires were stripped or burned and
sank within the century.

The wars ended at the **Peace of the Shoals**, which invented the charter
system still used in the Veyl Reefs: lane-rights become contracts, contracts
can be sold, and anything that can be sold need not be fought over. Cynics
note the Peace was brokered by the Deepway, who by then were burying the war
dead of every side and simply announced they would stop.

## The Marrow Rush (590–700 A.S.)

A Roak artificer of Hollow Vesse, Pellin Graveswright, demonstrated in 591
that marrow-breath could be **tapped, bottled, and burned** — portable lift,
portable light. Within a generation the Root-Dark of every major spire was a
mine. Lamps, lift-barges, breath-bottles, the whole modern economy dates from
this century — and so does the first recorded **sinking of a settled spire**
(Cathlow, 648 A.S., stripped by its own syndics, 1,100 dead).

The Spirarchy's response created the modern regulatory world: tapping
licenses, lift-ledgers, the Ninth Gallery inspectorate. The Marrower Combine
dates its own founding charter, with no apparent irony, to the year after the
Cathlow sinking.

## The Long Calm (700–990 A.S.)

Three centuries the histories describe as golden and the lift-ledgers
describe as *slowly tilting*. Trade thickened; the Moult grew from a
migration into a nation; the Quillate perfected its sciences of weather,
lineage, and law. And threaded through the era's records, easy to dismiss
one entry at a time:

- Windworkers' guild minutes complaining the Breath answers "more slowly
  than in our masters' time" (first entry 743 A.S., then steadily).
- The Vael retiring "tired lanes" — windlanes whose schedules had grown
  unreliable. Two retired in the 700s. Five in the 800s. Eleven in the 900s.
- Carrowspar raising the funeral toll, twice, citing "the deepening of the
  way."
- Average lamp-burn per bottle of marrowgas, meticulously recorded by the
  Lanternfall craft-houses, declining about one percent per decade for as
  long as the series runs.

No one assembled the series. Assembling such series is what the Quillate is
for, which makes the not-assembling its own kind of record.

## The Stilling Age (990 A.S. – present)

In 990, courier Sefa Half-Light hit dead air on the Greywake lane and lived
to report it. The rest the world knows: the first **Stilling** swallowed the
lane, then in 995 took Greywake Spire itself and nine thousand lives — the
worst single calamity in recorded history. There are now at least seven
zones, all growing.

The decade since has been the modern political landscape forming under
pressure: the Spirarchy criminalizing "still-panic"; the Gale Wardens
chartered (officially a survey-and-rescue service; actually the only body
allowed to say the word *accelerating* in writing); the Deepway's diving
moratorium (996); the Hushed Choir surfacing from rumor into the open; the
High Quill sealing the deepest archive (999) by his own hand and posting his
own guards.

In the new year 1001 — the season of the High Gales — the Lanternfall lane
began to die, and the Wardens were ordered to get three thousand
lamp-crafters out. Which is where the story starts.

## Appendix: The Skeptic's Chronology

What a sharp-eyed character can assemble *today* from public sources — the
list the Quillate has never compiled in one place:

1. The Record begins abruptly at Year 0, edges clean. (Archive, verifiable.)
2. All three kindred song-cycles fall silent over the same missing decades.
3. The Breath has measurably weakened along a smooth curve since at least
   700 A.S. — centuries before the first Stilling. (Lamp-burn series,
   guild minutes, retired lanes.)
4. The institutions of all three founding lineages have, at every historical
   junction, acted to keep the pre-Settling years closed.
5. The Deepway's funeral chant — *"we return what was taken; we do not
   return enough"* — is older than the secrecy, and is an accounting term.

The conclusion the list points at is never stated in the Record. It is the
campaign's central revelation, and characters are meant to arrive at it the
hard way.
