Monochrome ink engraving, antique steel-plate book-illustration style in the manner of Gustave Doré: black ink on foxed off-white paper, fine burin lines, dense cross-hatching, stipple for soft forms, high-contrast chiaroscuro. A single dramatic plate.

Subject: nine robed figures in wax-bleached vestments stand evenly spaced on a pale reef of bone adrift in a dead-calm sky, all turned the same way, mouths open in a low unison song. Around and above them drift pale, translucent, bell-shaped forms about the size of a crouching person, sinking slowly to settle over the singers' upturned faces. The air about them is unnaturally still — render this dead air as flat, empty, untextured paper, void of the stippled cloud and mist-grain that fill the living sky beyond. In the near foreground, half in shadow behind a knuckle of bone, two winged Wardens — feathered humanoids with folded wings — crouch low and watch, unseen.

Mood: hushed dread and wrongness; a sinister stillness, beauty turned to threat. Deep blacks, luminous untouched paper for the pale bells and the dead air.

No colour. No lettering inside the image. No modern elements. The figures are winged people, not bird-headed and not angelic.
