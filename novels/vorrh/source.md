# Source Material — The Veilwrack: The Stilling

## Dramatis Personae

### Kithrel of the Moult <!-- id: myth-char-07de1099b49c -->

Vael courier turned Gale Warden; the fastest survey-flier in the company

Kithrel flew the Greywake windlane as a fledgling courier and was off-shift the day the Spire sank. Everyone she trained with was not. She joined the Wardens the next season and has flown survey on every new Stilling since, charting dead air edges closer than regulations allow. She keeps her dead flight's lane-tokens braided into her primaries.

### The Hushed Lampwright <!-- id: myth-char-3244f2daf6c4 -->

Tilve Wickmend - Vael lamp-tender of Lanternfall's western face, nine years on the lamps; betrothed of Hollm, the Deepway diver lost below the skim. Went down past the root-line on a tether, came up silent, and has not spoken or written since. Still lights every lamp, on schedule.

GM: Tilve Wickmend is the campaign's first on-screen Quieting-in-progress, and the keeper of Vorrh's debt - she was never told the truth about Hollm's death; the Synod's record says 'carried down, complete, sealed.' Vorrh carries Hollm's spliced sinew betrothal ring (with its finger-bone, wrapped in cold-wax). WHAT HAPPENED: checking the lowest lamp-ring she found the skim risen ~200m above chart and a still pocket forming against the spire's western root - and she heard, faintly, what Vorrh heard on his last sanctioned dive. Forty minutes inside the quiet edge began her Quieting; it is EARLY and arrestable - the rules question of the season is whether truth, grief named aloud, and living voices can re-anchor a person sliding into silence (treat as opposed Willpower aided by genuine human connection; the ring is a +20 anchor or a push, depending entirely on HOW the truth is given). Her lamps at the lowest ring burn faintly blue. She mends wicks others throw away. TIMELINE: untreated, she stops tending the upper rings within two nine-days, then walks down. PLAYER-FACING NAME: Tilve Wickmend; the draft has started calling her 'the hushed lampwright' when she can't hear, which is always, which is the problem.

### Talon Serra Redjess <!-- id: myth-char-5abdd75f4427 -->

Lanhawk Talon-captain of the Redjess clutch; the campaign's recurring corsair.

Vael, mid-thirties, a deed-name she will not explain. Her home spire was stripped to a husk by Combine crews under a lawful Crown charter; she watched it go down through the Undermist with her mother's tether still knotted to the dock. She holds a grudge like an heirloom and a genuine grievance, and she has decided the lawful world owes her a living. VOICE: courteous before violence, never after. GM ARC: fight her twice, bargain with her once, and keep her alive - by Act 4, when the lanes start dying under everyone equally, she is the ally nobody wants and everybody needs. She honors shrine-ground, the bier-right, and ransom custom without exception. LEVERAGE: the Combine charter that doomed her spire is in the Quillate's deep stacks, and the PCs can find it.

### Quiet Plume Avess <!-- id: myth-char-465906c7a38c -->

Senior Crown Agent; the courteous face of the archive suppression.

Roak, Recensor's line, sixty years in the Crown's service. Avess is not a thug; she is an editor. Her weapons are confiscation, charter revocation, the arrest of the PCs' allies, and the offer - always sincere, always poisoned - of legitimacy in exchange for silence. VOICE: tired courtesy; she apologizes for inconveniences she has caused on purpose. SECRET: she has read enough of what she suppresses to know the truth about the Stillings, and she is increasingly aware she is protecting the thing that will kill her grandchildren. GM ARC: convertible - at a price. Her passion 'Protect my grandchildren's sky' beats her duty passion in a fair contest; the PCs' job is to force that contest in front of her.

### Recensor Velute <!-- id: myth-char-911011aeef29 -->

The Spirarchy's keeper of the founding lie; author of the cruelest clean solution.

Roak, Recensor's line, weather - the line that has kept the founding secret for a thousand years. Velute knows what the founders did to Suruveil and believes, with real conviction, that a thousand years of order vindicates the silence. She is right about panic; let her say it well. GM ARC (Act 5): she will offer the Settling Right - requisition the Spindles, ten thousand unrecorded lungs the Record would never miss, to wake the Unsung. The offer comes in a quiet room, politely, with tea. She will also trade: truth-management for genuine help containing panic. Disposition track Spirarchy: starts Hostile.

### Archivist Orrocan <!-- id: myth-char-b66122db0da5 -->

High Quill of the deep stacks; terrified custodian of the self-filling shelves.

Roak, old, precise, frightened. Since the deep-stack fire last Fogs, Orrocan has known the worst thing an archivist can know: nothing was taken, but something has been ADDED, and the hand is not one he can identify. He suppresses the PCs' access because procedure is the only floor he has left. GM ARC: he WANTS to be forced - give the PCs a way to compel him that lets him keep his oath, and he becomes the best ally in the campaign. Quillate disposition: starts Obstructive; moves on this one man.

### Sister Ulvane <!-- id: myth-char-19383cc4f0da -->

Stairwarden of the Long Stair; the Synod's pragmatic edge.

Ossuin, one name, given at her first descent forty years ago. Ulvane has been deeper than anyone living and does not talk about it - but she has heard the nursery lights sing, and unlike the Synod she thinks the living should be told before the wax runs out. VOICE: short sentences, long silences, no metaphors (Ossuin are never being metaphorical). GM ARC: the door into the Deepway. She will defy the Synod exactly once, for people who have carried a body down properly. The un-buried dead crisis is her breaking point.

### Hssh <!-- id: myth-char-f69418c32f0a -->

Sotto Voce of the Choir; the faction of the cult that wants to help.

Ossuin, age unguessable, voice a whisper by choice and lately less by choice. Hssh has walked Stilling edges for thirty years and knows their craft better than any Warden analyst. When the Sotto Voce learns what the Unsung are, the Choir splits - and Hssh leads the faction that chooses the world. GM ARC: ally from Act 3 if treated with respect earlier; the source for Stilling survival craft the PCs cannot learn elsewhere. COUNTERPART: the other Choir faction's true believers decide hatching a new Worldlung would undo the holy quiet, and work sincerely toward the world's death. Killing sad, sincere pilgrims should feel terrible; make Hssh the reason it does.

### Brell Coinfeather <!-- id: myth-char-7a333939b3c5 -->

Roostless fixer and information broker; insurance is her religion.

Spindles-born, two-part name, which by Spindles reckoning means she is honest about what she is. Brell brokers everything: passage, papers, silence, and - crucially - she holds the insurance copy of the Marrower Combine's real sink-rate numbers for Hollow Vesse. She does not know yet what they are worth. GM ARC: the Combine's endgame breaks open when her copy goes public at exactly the right moment; until then every faction in the campaign has a reason to buy, befriend, or bury her. She survives by being more useful alive to everyone. Rumor 4 (the commissioned glider) is true: she is planning her own exit.

### Commander Esk Veil-Torn <!-- id: myth-char-090b0e0c6b34 -->

Warden Company-Commander of the Lanternfall draft; the PCs' patron.

Vael, deed-name earned at Greywake in 995 - she went into the Stilling after her wing-sister and came out alone, torn, carrying the body anyway. Esk commands the Lanternfall draft because no one else would take a dying lane. VOICE: economical, fair, allergic to speeches. She gives the PCs real autonomy and honest accounting. GM USE: patron, not quest dispenser - she tells the company what the Wardens know (including, eventually, the Acceleration Map) and asks what they intend. Her creed passion is the campaign's moral baseline; if the finale demands a price, she volunteers first.

### Vorrh <!-- id: myth-char-fa3f498d958e -->

Ossuin death-diver, cast out of the Deepway; an outcast who watches, asks unwelcome questions, and dives where no one is permitted.

VORRH (the sound the Undermist makes against a slow wing). Ossuin, named at his first descent - he went down too steeply, and when the stairwardens called it recklessness he said he was listening. Trained as a Death-Diver at Carrowspar: breath-bottles, cold-wax, the descent-chants, the only legal Undermist training in the sky. HOLLM: his stair-cohort brother, named at the same first descent ('the held breath'). Hollm was betrothed to a lamp-tender of Lanternfall Spire, the wedding set for the Wet. On Vorrh's last sanctioned dive a singing came up through the gold that was not the cohort's chant; Vorrh held the count, but Hollm turned toward it, folded his wings, went below the skim, and did not come up. The Synod's record of that day reads 'Carried down, complete, sealed' - a proper funeral for a death no one witnessed, of a body no one carried. Vorrh asked how the record could be complete when he was there and it wasn't. He was ordered to unhear the singing. He asked again. That was the exile: the Deepway cannot take his name back, but they will no longer say it. KEEPSAKE - HOLLM'S RING: months later, alone and unsanctioned, Vorrh found Hollm snagged in reef-bone at the skim, one arm raised toward the surface, the spliced sinew betrothal ring (broken once, mended toward her) still on his finger. Vorrh carries the ring and its finger-bone wrapped in cold-wax. The unfulfilled need is not his own: somewhere on Lanternfall is a lamp-tender who was handed a sealed lie instead of a husband and was never told the truth. That is why, of all the dying lanes in the sky, Vorrh answered the posting at Lanternfall. GM NOTE: the lamp-tender is an undefined NPC - name and circumstances open; the Deepway's false record of Hollm's death is a live thread into the Synod's secrets (what sang, and why the Synod papers over skim-losses). UPBRINGING: What My Priest Told Me (Ossuin catechism) - he believed it all once; the exile is partly about what happens to that belief. PASSIONS: Yearning 59 (to hear again what he heard below); Duty 49 (the dead go down, all of them, even his); Fear 39 (that the dark is answering back). Kindred bonus: +5 Endurance. ARRIVING: Lanternfall Spire, High Gales 1001 A.S., seeking hire with Commander Esk's Warden draft.

## Locations

### Suruveil's Crown <!-- id: myth-loc-9bad0f0a6a51 -->

Capital of the Veilwrack; the largest Spire in the world, visibly a titanic horned skull in low light.

Seat of the Spirarchy and home of the Quillate archive-guild. Population about 200,000 Alar of all three kindreds. The sealed Tenth Gallery of the palace contains the Founding Compact. Marrower gangs work the spire-roots below; the upper galleries are Roak guild-halls; the Moult is currently anchored windward.

### The Moult <!-- id: myth-loc-bececa624d19 -->

The Vael nation: a migrating flotilla of kite-rafts and tethered gliders, thousands strong.

Anchored windward of the Crown after abandoning two ancestral windlanes to Stillings. Mood: grief masked as restlessness. The lane-charts the Moult keeps are the best Stilling data in the world, and the Spirarchy wants them.

### Greywake <!-- id: myth-loc-a2220bb0911b -->

The drowned Spire: sank into the Undermist five years ago at the heart of the oldest Stilling. Nine thousand died.

Its top hundred feet still jut from the cloud-sea. The Hushed roost there in their hundreds, a silent congregation. Holds a Compact-era killing harpoon and the first whole copy of the Founding Compact. Stilling Potency 80.

### Lanternfall Spire <!-- id: myth-loc-f3c849a67a27 -->

A roost-spire of about three thousand, mostly Roak lamp-crafters. Its windlane began Stilling this season.

Intro adventure site: evacuation under pressure. The lane-shrine beacon-horn still works. A Stillwight hunts the dead stretch of lane, and a Hushed child follows evacuees out — it remembers its mother, who is among them.

## Factions

### The Gale Wardens <!-- id: myth-faction-e44c881ec80e -->

Rangers, rescue-fliers, and Stilling-surveyors. The player characters' company.

Part rescue service, part research expedition, part heresy in feathers. Commander: Esk Veil-Torn (Vael, f), who lost her wing-sister at Greywake. Field data shows the Stillings are accelerating and are not random; the Spirarchy suppresses this.

### The Spirarchy of Suruveil's Crown <!-- id: myth-faction-ecf80a4d4291 -->

Ruling council of the capital. Order, continuity, legitimacy.

Led by Spirarch Velute the Ninth: not a villain, a custodian of an inherited lie she believes necessary. Punishes still-panic as sedition. Keeps the Founding Compact sealed in the Tenth Gallery.

### The Quillate <!-- id: myth-faction-0e3bbc3af445 -->

The Roak archive-guild: the longest unbroken written record in the world.

The Record has a hole exactly one thousand years deep, cut deliberately. High Quill Orrocan knows the hole exists and is terrified of what unsealing it does to Roak legitimacy. Potential ally.

### The Hushed Choir <!-- id: myth-faction-42b455eaf8a4 -->

A cult that worships the Stilling as the world's overdue sabbath. They walk into still zones singing.

Partially right about everything. Their interlocutor Cantor Hssh (a Hushed, formerly Ossuin) writes beautifully and wants the PCs to understand the Stilling is the world breathing in. They know a working Windworker spending MP daily makes a Stilling recede.

### The Marrowers <!-- id: myth-faction-edc614ec0ad1 -->

Industrial guild tapping spire-marrow breath as lift-gas. Profitable. Catastrophic.

Tapped spires sink measurably faster. They ran the numbers; they burned the numbers. Magnate Brell Coinfeather still has a copy and is buyable, once.

### The Lanhawk Mews <!-- id: myth-faction-863dfd0fba02 -->

The loose council of windlane pirate clutches out of the Veyl Reefs.

Independent clutches of 6-20 wings under Talon-captains; the Mews meets each Long Calm to divide lane territories and arbitrate clutch feuds. Negotiable: Lanhawks honor shrine-ground, the bier-right, and ransom custom, and they flog tether-cutters like everyone else. They prey on Combine barges by preference and Crown couriers by necessity. GM: use them as the campaign's recurring, bargainable human-scale threat - and as a desperate ally pool by Act 4, when the lanes everyone shares begin to die.

### The Deepway of Carrowspar <!-- id: myth-faction-9daff6cde1c9 -->

The Ossuin funerary order: keepers of the descent, the wax, and the Long Stair.

The Deepway holds the funeral right - the monopoly on carrying the dead down to the Undermist - and answers to the Synod of Nine Silences. Its silence is institutional: the Synod knows more about what waits below than it will say, and its mist-pearl buy-back is not piety. GM: disposition track starts Obstructive; Sister Ulvane is the door. The Synod's deadline is real - they know time is shorter than secrecy.

## Lore (player-visible)

### The World of the Veilwrack <!-- id: myth-lore-e50782bd58d0 -->

No ground; an endless sky over the Undermist. Civilization lives on Spires: floating husks of dead sky-leviathans. The wind (the Breath) is alive.

## The World

The Veilwrack is an ocean of open sky. Far below everything lies the
**Undermist**: a luminous, slow-boiling cloud-sea, faintly gold by day and
green-lit from within by night. Nothing that descends into the Undermist
returns, save the Ossuin death-divers — and they only skim its surface, and
they do not speak of what they hear down there.

There is no land. Civilization clings to the **Spires** — colossal hollow
towers of pale, porous bone-stone, some a half-mile tall, that float at
altitude like anchored islands. The learned know what the common roost-folk
prefer to forget: the Spires are not stone. They are the calcified husks of
the **Anemoi**, the sky-leviathans of the elder world — vast coiling beings,
part whale and part weather-front, whose marrow still holds *breath*: living
wind, trapped in the bone, which keeps each husk aloft.

The wind of the Veilwrack is not mere moving air. It is the **Breath** — a
living medium. Thermals behave with intent; trade-gales run on schedules the
Vael have charted for forty generations; a still day is an omen. Every Alar
culture has its own name for the Breath and its own way of listening to it.

### Alar Anatomy and Body Plan <!-- id: myth-lore-4312506d0aa3 -->

Six-limbed winged humanoids: legs, arms, AND wings. Nine hit locations; wings are the tactical vulnerability; armor torso-and-head only.

## Alar Anatomy and Body Plan

The Alar are winged humanoids with a six-limbed body plan: two legs, two
arms, AND two wings (the angel configuration, not the harpy one -- arms and
wings are separate limbs, so an Alar can fight, craft, and carry while
flying).

Layout, top to bottom: a beaked, keen-eyed head; two large shoulder-mounted
wings rising from the upper back above and behind the arms; two fully
functional arms with hands; a keeled chest anchoring the flight muscle; an
abdomen; and two legs ending in clawed feet that grip and climb bone-stone
at full walking speed.

Combat consequences (avian hit location table, 1d20): 1-2 R Leg, 3-4 L Leg,
5-7 Abdomen, 8-10 Chest, 11-12 R Wing, 13-14 L Wing, 15-16 R Arm,
17-18 L Arm, 19-20 Head. Nine locations versus a human's seven. Wings are
the great tactical vulnerability: a Serious Wound to a wing (failed
Endurance contest) means no flight and triggers Grounded Dread; a Major
Wound to a wing while airborne is usually fatal at altitude. Armor doctrine
follows the anatomy: torso and head only, wings always bare.

Physique: hollow-boned and slight (SIZ -3, DEX +2 vs human baseline);
knockback against an Alar is doubled; a Flight roll halves falling distance
(arrests it entirely on a critical). Kindred variation: Vael slightest and
fastest, Roak middle-built, Ossuin +2 SIZ -- big heavy gliders built for the
long descent.

### The Three Kindreds <!-- id: myth-lore-607ae3902d04 -->

Vael (kestrel couriers, Nomadic), Roak (corvid archivists, Civilized), Ossuin (condor death-priests, Barbarian) - and what each believes the Breath is.

## The Alar

The winged peoples of the Veilwrack call themselves the **Alar**. They are
human-sized avianoids — hollow-boned, keen-eyed, feathered from crown to
tail, with strong arms beneath their wings and clawed, dexterous feet. An
Alar in flight is the master of three dimensions; an Alar grounded is a
person halved. There are three great kindreds:

### The Vael (kestrel-kin)
Swift, slight, restless. The Vael are the couriers, wind-pilots, and traders
of the Veilwrack, living their whole lives along the **windlanes** — the great
trade-gales between Spires. Their nation is **the Moult**, a migrating
flotilla of kite-rafts and tethered gliders thousands strong. They believe
the Breath is a herd, and that it can be *followed* but never owned.
**Culture: Nomadic.** Typical careers: Courier, Wind-pilot, Merchant, Scout.

### The Roak (corvid-kin)
Black-feathered, deliberate, insatiably curious. The Roak are the
memory-keepers, artificers, and archivists. Their guild-city occupies the
upper galleries of Suruveil's Crown, and their great institution, the
**Quillate**, holds the longest unbroken written record in the world —
though even the Quillate's oldest scrolls go strangely silent about the
years before the Crown was settled. The Roak believe the Breath is a
*language*, and that whoever finally parses its grammar will understand
everything. **Culture: Civilized.** Typical careers: Scholar, Crafter,
Official, Physician.

### The Ossuin (condor-kin)
Huge, patient, grave. The Ossuin are the death-priests and Undermist-divers,
the only caste permitted by ancient compact to descend below the Spire-roots.
They carry the dead down to the cloud-sea and return with **mist-pearls** —
condensed droplets of the Undermist that the other kindreds prize and fear in
equal measure. The Ossuin believe the Breath is an *inheritance* — borrowed,
not given — and their oldest funeral chant contains the line that makes Roak
archivists deeply uncomfortable: *"We return what was taken; we do not return
enough."* **Culture: Barbarian.** Typical careers: Priest, Hunter, Physician,
Warrior.

### Alar characteristics (character creation)
Roll as standard Mythras (3d6 / 2d6+6) then apply: **SIZ −3 (min 4), DEX +2**.
Movement: 4m walking, 12m flying. **Flight (STR+DEX)** is a Standard skill;
Swim is not (an Alar in water is in serious trouble). Hit locations use the
avian table (see rules/combat.md). Vael add +5 DEX-based skill of choice;
Roak add +5 INT-based; Ossuin add +5 to Endurance or Willpower.

### The Stilling <!-- id: myth-lore-3ac57167d76c -->

Spreading zones of dead air: flight fails, sound dies, Windworking stops, spires sink. Home of Stillwights and the Hushed.

## The Threat: The Stilling

Eleven years ago, a Vael courier flying the Greywake windlane hit air that
did not move. Not calm air — *dead* air: thick, soundless, syrup-slow, where
wings find no purchase and lanterns burn low and blue. She barely glided
clear. Within a season the dead zone had swallowed the whole windlane. Within
five years it had reached Greywake Spire itself — and Greywake, its marrow
breath gone quiet, **sank**, screaming and crumbling, into the Undermist,
taking nine thousand Alar with it.

The zones are called **Stillings**. There are now at least seven, and they
are growing. Inside a Stilling:

- **Flight fails.** Each round of attempted flight in still air requires a
  Flight roll (Hard); failure accrues a level of Fatigue. Gliding is
  impossible; there are no thermals. Most who enter walk out along
  spire-bridges — or do not come out.
- **Sound dies.** Voices carry no more than arm's length. All Perception
  by hearing is Herculean. Alar instinctively hate this more than anything.
- **The Breath is absent.** Windworking (the Alar magic of shaped air) does
  not function at all.
- **Things live there now.** The Stillwights — drifting, glassy predators
  that drink the moving air out of living lungs. And worse: the **Hushed** —
  Alar who were caught in a Stilling and came back changed. Silent. Wrong.
  Breathing *out* quiet the way a fire breathes out smoke.

No one knows what causes a Stilling, how to predict one, or how to stop one.
The Spirarchy of Suruveil's Crown insists the zones are natural weather and
punishes "still-panic" as sedition. The Vael have begun rerouting the
windlanes. The Ossuin have stopped accepting new diving commissions and will
not say why. And the Quillate's deepest archive has been sealed by order of
its own High Quill.

### Key Locations <!-- id: myth-lore-f4c60b03fffc -->

Suruveil's Crown, the Moult, Greywake, the Windlanes, the Spire-roots.

## Key Locations

- **Suruveil's Crown** — the capital: the largest Spire in the world, visibly
  a titanic horned skull when the light is low, which polite society does not
  mention. Seat of the Spirarchy; home of the Quillate; population ~200,000.
- **The Moult** — the Vael raft-flotilla, currently anchored windward of the
  Crown after abandoning two ancestral windlanes to Stillings.
- **Greywake** — the drowned Spire. Its uppermost hundred feet still jut from
  the Undermist at the heart of the oldest Stilling. The Hushed roost there.
  The Wardens want a survey expedition; nobody sane volunteers.
- **The Windlanes** — the living trade-gales. Lane-shrines along each route
  hold beacon-horns and emergency roosts.
- **The Spire-roots** — the dangling, root-like bone tangles beneath each
  Spire, where marrow-breath seeps and Marrower gangs work in the dark.

### Alar Racial Abilities <!-- id: myth-lore-2502bf9de47e -->

Wings, hollow bones, keen eyes, Grounded Dread, no swimmers; kindred bonuses.

## Alar Racial Abilities (all kindreds)

- **Wings.** Flight (STR+DEX) is a Standard skill. Walk 4m, fly 12m.
- **Hollow bones.** SIZ −3 (min 4), DEX +2 at creation. Knockback distances
  against an Alar are doubled; falling damage is rolled normally but a Flight
  roll (Hard if a wing is wounded) halves effective fall distance — fully
  arrested on a critical.
- **Keen eyes.** Perception rolls relying on sight at long distance are one
  grade easier. Perception by smell is one grade harder.
- **Grounded dread.** An Alar who cannot fly (wing wound, Stilling, confined
  space) suffers Willpower rolls one grade harder against fear and panic
  effects. This is cultural as much as physical: flightlessness is the
  Veilwrack's image of death.
- **No swimmers.** Swim is a Professional skill for Alar; feathers waterlog
  in one round, after which Flight is impossible until dried (an hour by fire).

### Kindred bonuses
- **Vael:** +5 to one DEX-based skill; start with one extra lane-token
  contact (a named NPC along any windlane).
- **Roak:** +5 to one INT-based skill; literate by default (Literacy at base
  INT×2 free).
- **Ossuin:** +5 to Endurance *or* Willpower; may take **Mist-Rites**
  (see careers) without a teacher; +2 SIZ (partially offsetting the Alar −3).

## Windworking (Magic)

Windworking is the Mythras Imperative **Magic** system (SRD: Magic and
Powers), re-expressed: the caster coaxes the living Breath into small
intentional shapes. **Skill: Windworking (POW+CHA)** — a Professional skill
requiring a teacher; it fills the role of the SRD Magic skill exactly.

**Casting (SRD rules):** one Action + a Windworking roll.

| Roll result | Magic Points | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| Critical | 0 MP | spell works |
| Success | 1 MP | spell works |
| Failure | 1 MP | spell fails |
| Fumble | 1d3 MP | spell fails AND the Breath answers wrong (GM complication: a backdraft, a dead hush, a whisper to the wrong ear) |

### Aerial Movement Rules <!-- id: myth-lore-4654f7d6411d -->

## Movement & Aerial Rules

| Mode | Rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Walk | 4m | claw-feet: climbing bone-stone at full rate, no roll, if unencumbered |
| Fly (cruise) | 12m | sustainable for hours along a lane wind |
| Fly (run gait) | 36m | Athletics/Flight roll per minute or +1 Fatigue |
| Fly (sprint/stoop) | 60m | combat bursts and dives; one roll per round of sustained sprint |
| Hover | 0m | Flight roll each round; failure = +1 Fatigue. Hummingbird work is HARD for a person-sized wing. |
| Glide | 12m, −1m altitude per 10m | effortless; impossible in a Stilling |

- **Altitude bands** (GM shorthand): Deck (0–10m, melee possible with
  grounded foes), Low (10–100m), Lane (100m–1km, where the trade-gales run),
  High (above 1km: cold, thin — Endurance roll per hour), and the Roots
  (below the spires, turbulent — Flight rolls to maneuver).
- **Carrying aloft:** an Alar can fly carrying up to STR in 'Things';
  each Thing beyond that imposes one grade on Flight and −2m fly speed.
  Carrying another person requires double their SIZ in effective STR —
  in practice, two fliers and a sling.
- **Armor aloft:** flying with more than 12 total worn AP requires a Flight
  roll per scene; the highest worn AP still penalizes Initiative. Alar armor
  doctrine: torso and head only, wings bare.
- **Aerial engagement:** both fliers engaged in melee descend ~5m per round
  of fighting unless one spends an Action Point on Move to hold altitude.
  Trip becomes **Tumble** (1d10m altitude loss + Flight roll); Bash shoves
  in any of six directions.

### Mythras Magic and Powers Rules <!-- id: myth-lore-4e47eaf277ba -->

Setting-agnostic SRD reference: Magic casting economy (crit 0 / success 1 / fail 1 / fumble 1d3 MP), spell traits, full SRD spell list, and the Superpowers build framework (core powers, Boosts, Limits).

# Mythras Imperative — Magic & Powers Reference

Distilled from the Mythras Imperative SRD chapter "Magic and Powers."
Two separate frameworks: **Magic** (spells, for magicians) and
**Superpowers** (core powers + Boosts + Limits, for super-beings).
Settings rename and re-flavor these (e.g. the Veilwrack's Windworking is
the Magic system verbatim — see setting/alar-options.md).

## Magic

**Skill:** Magic (POW+CHA), a Professional skill. No skill, no magic.
**Pool:** Magic Points = POW. Recovery: Healing Rate per hour of full rest.

**Casting:** one Action + a Magic skill roll:

| Roll | MP cost | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Critical | 0 | spell works |
| Success | 1 | spell works |
| Failure | 1 | spell fails |
| Fumble | 1d3 | spell fails (GM may add a mishap) |

All spells are Intensity/Magnitude 1 (petty magic). Duration: the scene,
unless the spell is *Instant*, *Concentration*, or has a special duration.

**Traits:**
- *Instant* — happens immediately, no duration
- *Concentration* — lasts while the caster concentrates undisturbed
- *Ranged* — up to Magic skill score in metres; unseen targets = one grade harder
- *Touch* — physical contact required (unwilling targets generally need Grip/surprise)
- *Resist (Endurance/Evade/Willpower)* — opposed roll vs the casting result;
  Evade resists cost the target an Action Point

**Starting spells:** 1d4+1. **Learning:** 3 Experience Rolls + one week +
a teacher or reliable source.

**Counter-magic in combat:** Avert (or setting equivalent) via the Counter
Spell reactive action; held spells use the Hold Magic action.

### SRD spell list (traits)

Alarm (special duration) · Avert (Instant, Ranged) · Befuddle (Ranged,
Resist Willpower) · Bladesharp (Touch: +1 damage die step, edged) ·
Bludgeon (Touch: as Bladesharp, blunt) · Breath (Touch: hold air POW/2
minutes) · Calm (Ranged, Resist Willpower) · Chill (Instant, Touch) ·
Darkness (Concentration, Ranged: POW sq m shadow) · Disruption (Instant,
Ranged, Resist Endurance: 1d3 ignoring armor) · Extinguish (Instant,
Ranged) · Find X (Concentration, Ranged, Resist special) · Firearrow
(Touch: missiles +1d3 fire) · Fireblade (Touch: melee +1d3 fire) · Glue
(Touch: Brawn POW×5 bond) · Heal (Instant, Touch: minor wounds fully
restored; serious/major stabilized only) · Ignite (Instant, Ranged) ·
Knock (Instant, Touch) · Light (Concentration, Ranged) · Lock (Touch,
special: MP doesn't recover while active) · Phantasm (Concentration,
Ranged) · Sleep (Touch, Resist Endurance: POW/2 hours; fails in combat) ·
Vigor (Touch: suspend exertion Fatigue) · Witchsight (Ranged, Resist
Willpower: see magic/invisible/true forms)

## Superpowers

**Pool:** Power Points = POW. Recovery: 1 per minute of rest, or spend a
Luck Point for 1d4+1 immediately. Core powers are always-on or at-will and
free; **Boosts** cost Power Points.

**Build:** core powers = POW + (CON for altered physiology | INT for
tech/training), per table: ≤12 → 1, 13-20 → 2, 21-30 → 3, 31-40 → 4,
40+ → 5. Trade one core power for two Boosts. Up to two **Limits**: one
Limit = bonus Boost; two Limits = bonus core power.

**Core powers (summary):** Animation (constructs from material) · Combat
Expert · Durability (HP from CON+SIZ+POW) · Elemental Control · Energy
Blast · Enhanced Reactions · Enhanced Speed · Enhanced Strength · Flight
(POW m move) · Heightened Sense · Inherent Armor (CON/3 all locations) ·
Life Support (immune to 2 environmental conditions) · Lucky · Regeneration
(1 HP/round to non-Major wounds) · Savant (3 skills one grade easier) ·
Teleport · Vaporous Form

**Limits:** Activation Cost · External Power Source · Fatal Flaw · Limited
Control (fumble on 90-00) · Limited Power (conditional) · Physical
Limitation · Vulnerability (double damage from X)

## GM guidance

- Use **Magic** for the setting's learnable tradition (folk-scale, costed,
  resistible). Use **Superpowers** for beings whose nature IS the power —
  monsters, the touched, the transformed. Don't mix frameworks on one
  character without good reason.
- Spell saturation: magic at this scale changes daily life (Heal, Vigor,
  Light) more than battles. Let NPC casters exist in the economy.
- Resist rolls use the CLI: `roll-opposed` with the caster's casting result
  re-entered as a flat skill if needed, or roll the target's resist via
  `roll-skill` against the cast quality narratively.

### The Shape of the World: Altitude Bands <!-- id: myth-lore-ff6e2f71f398 -->

The five altitude bands - Glare, Reach, Shade, Root-Dark, Undermist - and the charted lanes vs the open Wrack.

## The Shape of the World

The Veilwrack is a band of habitable sky roughly four miles deep, wrapped
around a world no living Alar has ever touched. Everything an Alar will ever
stand on floats.

**The altitude bands**, as every fledgling learns them:

- **The Glare** (above ~5 miles): thin, frigid, brilliant air. Unbreathable
  for more than minutes. Only the high gales and the sun live there. Reckless
  Vael racers "kiss the Glare" as a rite of passage; some come back.
- **The Reach** (3–5 miles): the upper habitable band. Cold, clean, fast air.
  Spire crowns, watch-roosts, the great windlanes. Most civic life happens
  here. Breath-pressure is comfortable; fires burn honest orange.
- **The Shade** (1–3 miles): warm, wet, cloud-streaked air below the spire
  waists. Skyfarms, rain-catches, drift-krill swarms, and most of the world's
  food. Also most of the world's predators.
- **The Root-Dark** (0–1 mile above the Undermist): hot, heavy, dim. The
  dangling spire-roots, the Marrower works, the Ossuin descent-stations.
  Lanterns burn low and red. Sound carries strangely. Unlicensed descent
  below the root-line is a crime nearly everywhere.
- **The Undermist** (below): the cloud-sea. Golden by day, green-lit by
  night, lethal in ways the kindreds argue about. Only Ossuin death-divers
  go below the surface skim, and only briefly.

There are between sixty and ninety settled Spires, depending on what you
count as settled, threaded together by some forty charted windlanes. Beyond
the charted lanes is the **Wrack** proper — open sky, rogue weather, drifting
bone-reefs, and whatever the old charts mean by the marginal note *"no
returns."*

### The Great Polities <!-- id: myth-lore-bf701cf74bcb -->

Crown Dominion, the Moult, Carrowspar Synod, Veyl Reefs, Brightgyre Compact - governments, rulers, and how each holds power.

## The Great Polities

Power in the Veilwrack is power over three things: **lift** (marrow-breath
and spire integrity), **lanes** (who may ride which wind), and **the Record**
(who is owed what, and since when). Each great polity controls one and covets
the other two.

### The Crown Dominion (Suruveil's Crown and its client spires)

The largest state in the world: Suruveil's Crown itself (pop. ~200,000) plus
eleven client spires within two days' flight, bound by charters of fealty.
Governed by the **Spirarchy** — part monarchy, part church, part land
registry. The Spirarch (currently **Velute the Ninth**, Roak) rules from the
Tenth Gallery; beneath her, the **Gallery Lords** each administer one ring of
the Crown, from the glittering First Gallery (diplomacy, festivals, the
Quillate's halls) down to the smoke-dark Ninth (marrow-tapping, refuse,
debtors). Your gallery of residence is your legal class; moving up a gallery
takes a generation or a fortune.

The Spirarchy's legitimacy rests on the **Settling Right**: the doctrine that
the founding lineages were *granted* the Crown by the dying elder world, and
that order aloft descends from that grant. Question the Settling Right in
public and you will meet the Crown Agents.

**Power structure:** Spirarch → Gallery Lords (9) → Wardens-of-Ring (civic
magistrates) → roost-reeves (block-level headmen). Parallel to all of it: the
**Crown Agents**, answerable to the Spirarch alone.

### The Moult (the Vael raft-nation)

Not a place — a *fleet*. Two to four thousand kite-rafts, tether-barges, and
woven gliders, lashed into shifting neighborhoods, following the seasonal
gales. The Moult anchors off a major spire for a season at a time, trading,
hiring out couriers, and absorbing the restless young of every roost it
passes.

Governed by the **Raft-Council**: one speaker per raft-cluster, meeting in
the open air, decisions made by *lift* — councilors literally rise from their
perches to vote, and a motion passes when more wing-area is airborne for than
against. Above the Council in moral authority (though holding no formal
office) stand the **Lanemothers**: the senior pilots who hold the windlane
charts in memory. A Lanemother's refusal to fly a lane is, in practice, a
veto no Council vote can override.

The Moult recognizes no land law. Its one absolute statute is the **Tether
Rule**: whoever cuts a tether in anger answers to everyone the raft was
carrying.

### The Carrowspar Synod (the Ossuin holy spire)

Carrowspar is a lean, dark, south-hanging Spire two days windward of the
Crown, fissured top to bottom by the **Long Stair** — the carved descent-way
down which the Ossuin carry the dead of every kindred to the Undermist. It is
the seat of the **Deepway**, the Ossuin priesthood, and the only polity whose
authority every other polity acknowledges without a treaty: everybody dies,
and only the Deepway knows the way down.

Governed by the **Synod of Nine Silences** — nine senior priests who answer
questions in order of seniority, the most senior speaking least. Day-to-day
rule falls to the **Stairwardens**. Carrowspar mints no coin, fields no army,
and has never lost a negotiation that mattered.

Eleven years ago the Synod stopped accepting new diving commissions. They
have not explained. The backlog of un-descended dead, stored in wax and salt
in every spire's bone-cellars, is becoming a quiet, world-wide crisis.

### The Veyl Reefs (the Free Shoals)

A drowned-cluster of forty-odd small spires, bone-reefs, and tether-towns in
the world's warm east, where three windlanes braid together. No central
government; instead, the **Shoal Charters** — a web of mutual-defense and
toll-sharing compacts enforced by hired wings. The Reefs are where you go to
buy what the Crown bans, sell what the Moult won't carry, and hire wings who
don't ask questions. Pirate clutches (the **Lanhawks** worst among them)
winter in the unchartered outer reefs, and everyone pretends not to know
which tether-town fences their cargo.

### The Brightgyre Compact (the skyfarm league)

Where a permanent warm updraft (the **Gyre**) rises between four mid-sized
spires, the Alar have built the world's breadbasket: hundreds of acres of
netted skyweed terraces, krill-weirs, and rain-catch sails turning slowly in
the bright air. The four spires — Helsper, Aulia, Cantling, and Wrest — pool
sovereignty in the **Compact Table**, a council of farm-syndic delegates.
Brightgyre is rich, pacifist by policy, and utterly dependent on the Gyre
continuing to turn — which is why the Compact quietly funds the Gale Wardens
better than any other patron, and why its delegates wake sweating from dreams
of still air.

### Lesser Powers and Places <!-- id: myth-lore-69b18c5dc113 -->

Lanternfall, Greywake, the Spindles, Hollow Vesse, and the lane-shrine network.

## Lesser Powers and Places

- **Lanternfall Spire** — a single-industry roost (lamp-crafting; its
  beeswax-and-marrowgas lanterns light half the world) of ~3,000, whose
  windlane is now dying. The campaign's opening crisis.
- **Greywake** — the drowned Spire, jutting from the Undermist at the heart
  of the oldest Stilling. Sovereign territory of nobody; congregation of the
  Hushed.
- **The Spindles** — a column of stripped, sinking spire husks abandoned over
  the centuries, now a vertical shantytown of the roostless. The Crown calls
  it a hazard; ten thousand people call it home.
- **Hollow Vesse** — a dead spire purchased outright by the Marrower Combine
  and converted into the world's only marrow refinery. Armed, private, and
  measurably lower in the sky every year.
- **The lane-shrines** — hundreds of small fixed roosts along every windlane:
  beacon-horn, rain-tank, three days' shelter, and a shrine-cantor who sings
  the lane-songs at dawn. Lane-shrines are neutral ground under the oldest
  law there is. Even Lanhawks honor it. Mostly.

### How Power Works in the Veilwrack <!-- id: myth-lore-e860d9dc708a -->

The four currencies of power: lift, lanes, the Record, and the funeral right.

## How Power Actually Works

1. **Lift is wealth.** A spire that loses marrow-breath sinks. Every polity's
   deepest books are lift-ledgers: how much breath the bone holds, how fast
   it seeps, what it costs to keep the nurseries on the high floors. The
   Marrower Combine's leverage is that it alone can move lift from where it
   is to where it's paid for — and its crime is where the lift comes from.
2. **Lanes are law.** Toll-rights on a windlane are inherited, married,
   gambled, and murdered for. The Moult's Lanemothers, the Crown's charters,
   and the Reefs' Shoal Charters are three incompatible legal theories of who
   owns the wind; most wars in history have been lane-wars.
3. **The Record is legitimacy.** The Quillate's archive arbitrates every
   treaty, debt, and lineage claim in the world. Whoever the Record favors,
   wins eventually. This is why the thousand-year hole at the bottom of the
   Record is not a scholarly curiosity. It is a loaded weapon nobody has
   fired yet.
4. **The Deepway is the constant.** Funeral right is the one service that
   cannot be substituted, boycotted, or counterfeited. The Synod's silence
   about why the diving stopped is therefore the loudest sound in world
   politics.

### Distances and Travel <!-- id: myth-lore-ec800151992d -->

## Distances and Travel

Reckoned in **wind-days** (one day's flight for a laden courier riding a
lane, ~250 miles):

| Route | Wind-days | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Crown ↔ Carrowspar | 2 | the Mourner's Lane; always flyable, always cold |
| Crown ↔ Brightgyre | 3 | the Grain Lane; heaviest traffic in the world |
| Crown ↔ Veyl Reefs | 6–8 | three lane-changes; pirate risk past the Spindles |
| Crown ↔ Lanternfall | 4 | **lane dying — currently 9+ days by detour** |
| Crown ↔ Greywake | 5 | charted; nobody flies it; final lane-shrine abandoned |

Off-lane flying halves speed and doubles Fatigue; night flying is for

barges at a third of courier speed, which is why a sack of Brightgyre
krill-meal costs five times as much in the Reefs.

### A History of the Veilwrack <!-- id: myth-lore-9e79e8954a72 -->

# A HISTORY OF THE VEILWRACK
### As the Record tells it — and where the Record goes quiet

*All dates are reckoned **A.S.** — After the Settling, the founding of
Suruveil's Crown. The current year is 1001 A.S.*

---

## The Elder Sky (before the Record)

Of the world before the Settling, the Record holds almost nothing, and what
the kindreds hold instead is song.

The songs agree on this much: the elder sky was **crowded with giants**. The
Anemoi — sky-leviathans vast as weather — coiled through the high air, and
the winds of the world ran warm and strong in their wake. The Alar of that
age were roostless: small clutch-bands following the leviathans the way gulls
follow a ship, gleaning, riding their pressure-wakes, nesting in the drifting
husks of the giant dead. The Vael songs call it the Long Following and make
it sound like freedom. The Ossuin chants call it the Debt-Making and make it
sound like something else.

Why the giants dwindled, the songs do not agree — plague, age, a sky grown
too cold, a sin. By the last century of the elder age there were few, and
then there was one, and the songs of all three kindreds go carefully,
identically silent on what happened next.

## The Hole (c. −60 to 0 A.S.)

Here is the fact that respectable scholarship walks around: **the Record has
a hole in it.** The Quillate's archive, the proudest unbroken chronicle in
the world, *begins* in Year 0 with a fully-formed founding charter in three
mature scripts — and holds nothing before it. No drafts. No genealogies
running backward. Sixty-odd years of the late elder age, the lifetimes of the
very founders, are simply absent, and the edges of the absence are clean.
Documents that should reference those years have been re-copied with the
references gone; the re-copying itself dates to the first century A.S.

The official Quillate position is that the pre-Settling Alar were not yet
literate and there is nothing to miss. The official position is taught,
examined, and quietly disbelieved by every archivist good enough to be
trusted with the deep stacks.

## The Settling (Year 0)

In Year 0, the three kindreds together settled the greatest husk in the sky
— the colossal horned Spire now called Suruveil's Crown — and signed the
**Founding Compact**, the treaty that apportioned the world: lanes to the
Vael, the Record and the workshops to the Roak, the funeral right and the
deep places to the Ossuin. The Compact's original text is held in the
Spirarchy's sealed Tenth Gallery and has not been publicly read in eight
centuries; its terms are known only through the digest in the Record.

Every polity in the world traces its legitimacy to this single document
nobody may read.

## The Compact Era (1–290 A.S.)

Three centuries of settlement. The kindreds spread along the great lanes,
charting and naming, raising roosts on every sound husk. The lane-shrine
network was sung into place rope by rope. Carrowspar's Long Stair was carved
(a labor of ninety years). The population of the world perhaps tripled.

It was also the era that built the world's quiet machinery of forgetting:
the Quillate's first Grand Recension (in which "corrupted" early documents
were re-copied and the originals burned), the Spirarchy's codification of the
Settling Right, and the Deepway's closure of the Undermist to all but its own
divers — three institutions, three locks, one door.

## The Charter Wars (290–355 A.S.)

The first great breakdown. As the good husks filled, younger lineages
demanded lanes of their own; the Vael Lanemothers refused to re-divide what
the Compact had apportioned. Sixty years of raids, tether-cuttings, and
lane-blockades followed — the only full war the Veilwrack has ever fought,
and it killed lift as much as people: four spires were stripped or burned and
sank within the century.

The wars ended at the **Peace of the Shoals**, which invented the charter
system still used in the Veyl Reefs: lane-rights become contracts, contracts
can be sold, and anything that can be sold need not be fought over. Cynics
note the Peace was brokered by the Deepway, who by then were burying the war
dead of every side and simply announced they would stop.

## The Marrow Rush (590–700 A.S.)

A Roak artificer of Hollow Vesse, Pellin Graveswright, demonstrated in 591
that marrow-breath could be **tapped, bottled, and burned** — portable lift,
portable light. Within a generation the Root-Dark of every major spire was a
mine. Lamps, lift-barges, breath-bottles, the whole modern economy dates from
this century — and so does the first recorded **sinking of a settled spire**
(Cathlow, 648 A.S., stripped by its own syndics, 1,100 dead).

The Spirarchy's response created the modern regulatory world: tapping
licenses, lift-ledgers, the Ninth Gallery inspectorate. The Marrower Combine
dates its own founding charter, with no apparent irony, to the year after the
Cathlow sinking.

## The Long Calm (700–990 A.S.)

Three centuries the histories describe as golden and the lift-ledgers
describe as *slowly tilting*. Trade thickened; the Moult grew from a
migration into a nation; the Quillate perfected its sciences of weather,
lineage, and law. And threaded through the era's records, easy to dismiss
one entry at a time:

- Windworkers' guild minutes complaining the Breath answers "more slowly
  than in our masters' time" (first entry 743 A.S., then steadily).
- The Vael retiring "tired lanes" — windlanes whose schedules had grown
  unreliable. Two retired in the 700s. Five in the 800s. Eleven in the 900s.
- Carrowspar raising the funeral toll, twice, citing "the deepening of the
  way."
- Average lamp-burn per bottle of marrowgas, meticulously recorded by the
  Lanternfall craft-houses, declining about one percent per decade for as
  long as the series runs.

No one assembled the series. Assembling such series is what the Quillate is
for, which makes the not-assembling its own kind of record.

## The Stilling Age (990 A.S. – present)

In 990, courier Sefa Half-Light hit dead air on the Greywake lane and lived
to report it. The rest the world knows: the first **Stilling** swallowed the
lane, then in 995 took Greywake Spire itself and nine thousand lives — the
worst single calamity in recorded history. There are now at least seven
zones, all growing.

The decade since has been the modern political landscape forming under
pressure: the Spirarchy criminalizing "still-panic"; the Gale Wardens
chartered (officially a survey-and-rescue service; actually the only body
allowed to say the word *accelerating* in writing); the Deepway's diving
moratorium (996); the Hushed Choir surfacing from rumor into the open; the
High Quill sealing the deepest archive (999) by his own hand and posting his
own guards.

In the new year 1001 — the season of the High Gales — the Lanternfall lane
began to die, and the Wardens were ordered to get three thousand
lamp-crafters out. Which is where the story starts.

## Appendix: The Skeptic's Chronology

What a sharp-eyed character can assemble *today* from public sources — the
list the Quillate has never compiled in one place:

1. The Record begins abruptly at Year 0, edges clean. (Archive, verifiable.)
2. All three kindred song-cycles fall silent over the same missing decades.
3. The Breath has measurably weakened along a smooth curve since at least
   700 A.S. — centuries before the first Stilling. (Lamp-burn series,
   guild minutes, retired lanes.)
4. The institutions of all three founding lineages have, at every historical
   junction, acted to keep the pre-Settling years closed.
5. The Deepway's funeral chant — *"we return what was taken; we do not
   return enough"* — is older than the secrecy, and is an accounting term.

The conclusion the list points at is never stated in the Record. It is the
campaign's central revelation, and characters are meant to arrive at it the
hard way.

### What My Mother Told Me (Vael) <!-- id: myth-lore-85bbf5cfaeab -->

The Vael childhood catechism: the herd, the hoofprints of the dead, why we carry, how to regard the other kindreds.

## What My Mother Told Me (a Vael childhood, the Moult)

My mother told me this on the high tether, feet hooked in the same rope,
because Vael truths are told while holding on to something.

**Where the world came from.** In the beginning the sky was a herd, little
wing. The great ones — the Anemoi, the grandmother-winds — ran their circuits
and the air ran after them, the way dust runs after the flock. Everything
that flies learned flying from following them. We were the *followers*: first
behind the herd, gleaning, riding the warm wake. When the herd lay down — and
herds do lie down, all herds, this is the first sad thing you learn — they
left us their bones to stand on and their last running still going round and
round the world. The windlanes are hoofprints, child. We live in the
hoofprints of the dead.

**Why we die.** Because we are *small*, and small things are quick, and quick
is paid for. The great ones lived ten thousand years and turned slow as
seasons. We get sixty, and we turn inside a wingspan. Fair trade. When you
die the Ossuin carry you down — let them, pay them, don't haggle — because a
body is ballast and a soul is lift, and what's left of you wants to go *down*
to where the new winds are made, the way rain goes down to come back as
weather. Anyone who tells you the dead are gone has never flown a lane their
grandmother charted.

**Why we are here.** To *carry*, little wing. Word, cargo, kin, fire,
medicine — the world is alive exactly as long as things move through it.
Still air is dead air; a still person is a dead person with the paperwork
pending. You were not born to hold ground. There is no ground.

**The others.** The Roak? Respect them, charge them double. They think the
wind is writing and want to read it; the wind is *running* and wants to be
run with — but a Roak will mend your glider truer than your own sister, and
their word on paper holds when wingbeats fail. The Ossuin — hush, lower your
voice the way I am doing. We owe them. Don't ask what. When a priest of the
Stair asks you for passage, you fly them free, you fly them first, and you
do not ask them questions over open sky. The Crown-folk and their Spirarchy
are landlords of a thing nobody can own; smile, pay the toll, remember the
lane was ours before their grandfathers learned to count it. And the Hushed —
my mother stopped the rope-work when I asked, the only time she stopped —
*the Hushed are what happens when the world forgets to keep moving. Pity
them on paper. Flee them in person.*

### What My Father Told Me (Roak) <!-- id: myth-lore-6191406700f7 -->

The Roak childhood catechism: the missing first pages, nothing recorded is wholly dead, completing the Record.

## What My Father Told Me (a Roak childhood, Suruveil's Crown)

My father told me this in the gallery stacks, in his copying-voice, which is
quieter than his praying-voice and far more serious.

**Where the world came from.** We do not know, and that sentence, said
honestly, is the founding stone of everything your lineage has built. What we
*observe*: the world is a sphere of air around a deep we cannot sound; the
Spires are biological husks of a vanished megafauna; the wind exhibits
structure — grammar — that mere heating of air should not produce. The vulgar
say the wind is alive. The careful say the wind is *patterned*, and that
every pattern has an author, and that the author is not in evidence. Our
whole kindred is one long attempt to read a book whose first pages are
missing. — Yes. Missing. You will learn, when you are older and bonded to
the archive, that the missing pages are not a metaphor. Sit with that
discomfort. It is the family inheritance.

**Why we die.** Entropy, formally. The body is an archive of fire and the
fire un-copies itself. But hear the consolation of our craft: *nothing
recorded is wholly dead.* You will die; your hand will survive in the margins
of forty documents; your observations will outlive your observatory. The
Ossuin take the body down and we do not interfere, because their rite is
older than our skepticism and — note this, it is the kind of fact that
matters — *their rite has never once changed.* Unchanging testimony is the
most precious document there is.

**Why we are here.** To complete the Record. Not to rule it — leave that
appetite to the Spirarchy — to *complete* it. Every debt witnessed, every
star charted, every windlane's schedule taken down twice by independent
hands. The world is winding down, child; even the lamp-burn tables say so,
though we do not say it loudly. If it winds down to nothing, the Record is
the proof we were here. If it can be wound back up — then somewhere in the
Record is the instruction, and someone of ours had better be able to find it.

**The others.** The Vael are the Record's bloodstream; never let contempt
for their bookkeeping become contempt for their knowledge — a Lanemother
holds in memory what would take us nine volumes, error rate enviable. The
Ossuin are our opposite number: they keep one document, orally, perfectly,
and will not let us copy it. The day the Deepway opens its chant-vault to the
Quillate is the day the world's two halves of the truth meet — your
great-grandmother petitioned for it annually until they stopped returning her
letters. The Spirarchy: we staff it, we draft its laws, and we privately
know the Settling Right is a citation to a document in a sealed room. Serve
it anyway; order is the climate scholarship needs. And the Hushed — they are
a *data point*, the most important one in nine hundred years. A person
subtracted from the wind. Whatever was subtracted is what the wind *is*.
Someone must interview them properly before the soldiers and the cultists
spoil the sample. It will probably have to be one of us.

### What My Priest Told Me (Ossuin) <!-- id: myth-lore-8c7411f35a7a -->

The Ossuin childhood catechism: the world as exhalation, death as installment, keeping the door.

## What My Priest Told Me (an Ossuin childhood, Carrowspar)

The Stairwarden told me this on the Long Stair itself, on my seventh
descent, where the lamplight goes red and you can hear the Undermist turn
over below you like a sleeper.

**Where the world came from.** Breathe out. Now wait — feel that need, the
little death before you breathe in? Hold the feeling. That is the oldest
fact. The world is a breath that something breathed *out*, fledgling, and
all of us — wing, bone, lamp, song — live inside that exhalation. The great
ones breathed the world; the world is the shape of their generosity. We
nest in their bones. We light our lamps with their marrow. Say it plainly,
because our whole kindred's office is to say plain things slowly: *we live
inside the dead, on the goodness of the dead, and we have not said thank
you properly in a thousand years.*

**Why we die.** To pay. Death is not punishment and not accident; it is the
installment. Every living thing borrows breath at birth and returns it at
death, and the place of returning is below, where the mist keeps the
world's accounts. This is why we carry the dead down — every kindred's dead,
the beggar and the Spirarch at the same shoulder-height — and why the chant
says what it says. You will sing the line ten thousand times in your life:
*we return what was taken; we do not return enough.* When you are older you
will notice the line does not say *what was given*. Notice it quietly.

**Why we are here.** To keep the door. Between the living sky and the deep
that renews it there must be a kindred that goes down with clean hands and
comes back with shut mouths, or the door becomes a market — imagine
Marrowers in the Undermist; you will need no other image of sin. We are
heavy, slow, long-lived, hard to frighten: built for the office. Mind the
door, mind the debt, mind the dead. Everything else — the pearls, the
politics, the Synod's nine chairs — is scaffolding around those three
duties.

**The others.** Love them; they are why the door matters. The Vael, who pay
their dead down promptly and grieve in motion — when a Moult raft drops
tether and circles Carrowspar in silence, that is mourning done correctly.
The Roak, who want to *copy the chant* — refuse them gently, forever; some
documents are true only in a living mouth. The Crown, which believes it
rules us because we have never corrected it. And the Hushed. Fledgling, the
others fear the Hushed wrongly. They are not the debt collected. They are
the debt *defaulted* — what remains when the world can no longer pay its
side. The Synod stopped the diving because of what the last diver heard
below. I will not tell you what she heard. I will tell you the Synod did
not stop out of fear. We stopped the way a family stops spending when it
finally reads the ledger.

### What My Chief Told Me (the Roostless) <!-- id: myth-lore-0765a97d8837 -->

The Spindles childhood catechism: a secondhand world, the Thread-book, catching what falls.

## What My Chief Told Me (a Spindles childhood, the roostless)

Chief Ammet Threadbare told me this on the gleaning nets under Spindle
Seven, because that is where the children of nobody get their answers.

**Where the world came from.** Who knows, cub? The Vael say a herd, the
Roak say a book, the Ossuin say a breath, and every one of those stories
ends with *their* kindred holding something and ours holding the net. Here
is what your chief knows for certain: the world came from somewhere, and by
the time it got to us it was *secondhand*. We live in the spires nobody
wanted, fish the air nobody charted, burn the lamp-dregs. Whatever made the
world, it had heirs, and then it had us.

**Why we die.** Cold, mostly. Falls. Bad air in a stripped husk. The pretty
answers are for people who die in beds. But I'll give you the Spindles
answer too, the real one: we die *unrecorded*, and that is the part to be
angry about. The Quillate writes down every Crown merchant's sneeze; when
Spindle Nine shed its skin-quarter in the storm of '88, the Record gave it
one line. So we keep our own book — the Thread-book, every name, every
fall, sewn in knots because paper rots. You'll learn the knots. That's your
letters, cub. Nobody is forgotten off *my* nets.

**Why we are here.** To catch what falls. That's the work and the creed
both. The high galleries drop things — cargo, chances, people — and the
Spindles catch them, mend them, and sell them back. You are here because
somebody's grandmother caught somebody's grandfather in a net, and you'll
stay alive by being the kind of person others would dive after. Loyalty's
the only lift we own. Hold your people the way bone holds breath.

**The others.** The Crown is weather: real, dangerous, not personal —
don't curse at it, just rig for it. The Moult will feed you and never let
you forget the feeding; fly with them a season, learn everything, come
home. The Quillate is a locked door with the truth behind it — every
Spindles cub dreams of robbing it; the smart ones grow up to get *hired*
into it, which is the better robbery. The Deepway is the one debt we pay
first — they carried our dead down free in the year of the storm and wrote
nothing in any ledger about it, and the Spindles do not forget that the way
the Record forgets us. The Marrowers will offer you wages, cub, good ones,
and some year when the nets are thin you'll take them — just count what
they pay you against what they're draining out from under everyone, and
come home before the arithmetic makes you one of them. And the Hushed?
We're the only folk who've fed them, you know. Left bread on the dead-line
at Greywake fringe. It was gone in the morning, and *something* had
arranged the crumbs in a circle. Make of that what you like. Your chief
makes of it: even the emptied are somebody's people, and nobody else was
leaving bread.

### Natural Philosophy: The Quillate Consensus <!-- id: myth-lore-f31902a212b0 -->

The plausible physics: the planet, buoyant bone, the Undermist, and the Vital Circulation Hypothesis - the one biological miracle.

## The Quillate Consensus (player-facing)

### The world is a planet

The learned consensus, from star observation and horizon geometry: the
Veilwrack wraps a **large, deep-aired world** circling an ordinary sun. The
sun rises and sets; there are seasons (the High Gales, the Wet, the Long
Calm, the Mourning Fogs); there is a moon, small and fast, useful for night
navigation. Gravity is real and constant — slightly gentler, the Quillate's
pendulum-keepers insist, than "bone-weight reckoning" would predict, which
is part of why flight as large as an Alar is possible at all.

The habitable band is a matter of **air pressure and temperature**. Go too
high and the air thins and chills past use; descend and it thickens, warms,
and grows heavy until — at the Undermist — it crosses into something that
behaves as much like a sea as a sky. There is, presumably, a *bottom* far
below: rock or ocean under pressures no bone could bear. No one has seen it.
The world's surface, if it has one, is as theoretical to the Alar as the
back of the sky.

### Why the Spires float

No mysticism required: **buoyancy**. Spire bone is a rigid foam — millions
of sealed cells of pale ossein, each holding **marrow-breath**, a gas
lighter than air (the Lanternfall lamp-masters, who burn it daily, will tell
you it burns the way swamp-vapors burn, hot and clean). A Spire is, in
effect, a rigid airship grown by a living thing: the Anemoi were creatures
of buoyant bone, and their husks remain buoyant after death exactly as a
ship remains afloat after its crew dies.

This is also why Spires **sink**: the bone-cells seep, slowly by nature,
quickly when tapped. Lift lost is lift gone; the foam does not refill
itself. (The Marrower Combine's pamphlets say otherwise. The Combine's
pamphlets also say tapping is painless to property values.)

The Undermist's upper surface marks the altitude where the outside air grows
dense enough to carry even seep-weakened bone — which is why drowned spires
like Greywake do not fall forever but settle, wallowing, half-submerged in
the cloud-sea.

### What the Undermist is

The consensus: a **permanent global cloud-deck of dense, hot, wet air** —
the boundary layer where the deep atmosphere's heat meets the habitable
band's cool. Golden by day (dust and droplets in sunlight), green-glowing by
night (the Quillate says luminous microlife, the way some seas glow; the
Ossuin say nothing). It is survivable at the skim with preparation — heat,
pressure, and blindness being the killers, along with predators adapted to
swim-fly in air thick enough to swim in. Deeper than the skim, pressure and
temperature climb without mercy.

Mist-pearls — the Ossuin's condensed droplets — are, chemically, just
Undermist water with dissolved minerals and a faint persistent luminescence.
What unsettles people is not their chemistry.

### What the wind is — the one miracle

Here ordinary meteorology runs out, and honest scholarship says so plainly.

A planet's winds should arise from sunlight and spin: belts, cells, storms —
statistical weather. The Veilwrack *has* such winds. But riding on them is
something else: the **Breath** — the reliable, scheduled, almost-mannered
component of the wind. The windlanes run like rivers in the sky, decade
after decade. Thermals hold longer than heating explains.

The Quillate's best model, the **Vital Circulation Hypothesis**, is
accepted by most and spoken aloud by few, because of where it leads: the
Breath is **biological**. The Anemoi were not riders of the wind but its
*organs* — vast aerial filter-feeders whose metabolism (gas exchange on a
weather-front scale, heat shed in slow pulses) drove and regularized the
circulation of the entire habitable band, the way a forest makes its own
rain. A sky with Anemoi in it was a *tended* sky.

The hypothesis explains, with uncomfortable economy:

1. why the lanes are scheduled (they are the fossil circulation of vanished
   metabolism, sustained by stored heat and the residual breath of the
   husks);
2. why the Breath weakens smoothly century over century (stored anything
   runs down);
3. why Windworking works (Alar lungs and the Breath share one chemistry —
   the working is a conversation between living airs, which is why it costs
   the worker and fails in dead air);
4. and what a Stilling is: **not an invasion but an exhaustion** — a patch
   of sky where the living component of the air has finally run out,
   leaving only dead meteorology: thick, sluggish, sound-swallowing calm.

The hypothesis does not explain — and its authors decline in writing to
speculate — **where all the Anemoi went**, why a sky once full of its own
gardeners emptied, or why the emptying coincides so neatly with the
beginning of the Record. The hypothesis's final page is famous in the deep
stacks for its last sentence: *"The reader is invited to consult the
historical record; the author has, and prefers meteorology."*

### Small consistencies (for the table)

- **Sound in a Stilling:** dead-air zones are temperature-inverted and
  dense-layered; sound refracts down and away. Voices at arm's length,
  silence at ten paces. (And yes — it is worse than the physics strictly
  requires. The physics is doing its best.)
- **Fire:** burns orange and well in the Reach, low and blue in still or
  Root-Dark air (different gas mix, poorer convection). Lantern color is
  every traveler's first still-gauge.
- **Water:** rain falls from the Shade's cloud-streets; spires harvest it
  in catch-sails and bone cisterns. Nobody drinks the Undermist.
- **Food:** skyweed (aerial algae mats), drift-krill (gnat-sized gleaning
  swarms), roost-fowl, netted gliders-fish from the Shade. Brightgyre's
  terraces feed the world; a closed sky is a hungry sky.
- **Metal:** scarce and meteoric. "Storm-iron" is gathered from strike-fused
  reef-bone after lightning storms; most tools are bone, horn, lacquer, and
  woven sinew. A steel knife is an heirloom. (This is why the harpoons of
  legend were *engineered*, not forged — and why the Roak engineered them.)

### The Calendar and the Festivals <!-- id: myth-lore-4ce2f8b799e6 -->

Four wind-seasons, the nine-day week, First Lift, Tanksfull, the Patching, Lastlight, millennium dread.

## The Calendar and the Festivals

The year is told by winds, not months: four seasons, each ~90 days, counted
in **nines** (the nine-day week, one day per Synod silence, say the Ossuin;
one per gallery, says the Crown; because nine divides ninety, say the Roak).

- **The High Gales** (the year's start — now): cold, fast, brilliant air;
  courier season; the long-haul trading runs. Festival: **First Lift**, when
  every fledgling who learned to fly that year flies the circuit of their
  home spire while the whole roost drums.
- **The Wet**: the Shade's clouds climb; rain-catch season; the skyfarm
  harvest. Festival: **Tanksfull**, communal, soaked, and the traditional
  season for weddings ("marry when the water's in").
- **The Long Calm**: hot, slow air; barge season; repairs, festivals,
  scholarship. Festival: **the Patching** — gliders, rooves, and quarrels
  are all to be mended before the Calm ends. Public reconciliation is a
  civic duty; carrying a grudge across the Patching is genuinely shocking.
- **The Mourning Fogs**: the Undermist breathes up; visibility dies; flying
  is grim. The season of funerals — the year's dead, wax-kept, are carried
  down in convoy on the **Stairfall** days. Festival, if it is one:
  **Lastlight**, when every spire burns one lantern per death at the
  root-line, and the Undermist glows back.

Years are counted After the Settling (A.S.); the current year is 1001.
"Millennium dread" is a real cultural force — the round number and the
Stillings arrived together, and everyone noticed.

### Alar Names <!-- id: myth-lore-44c87e02566b -->

Naming customs of the Vael, Roak, Ossuin, and the roostless.

## Names

- **Vael:** short given name + deed-name or lane-name, earned and changeable:
  *Sefa Half-Light, Kithrel of the Moult, Esk Veil-Torn.* Asking how a Vael
  got their second name is polite; assuming, rude.
- **Roak:** given name + lineage name from the mother's archive-line:
  *Orrocan, Velute, Pellin Graveswright.* Roak introduce themselves with
  profession: "Velute, Recensor's line, weather."
- **Ossuin:** one name only, given at the first descent: *Ulvane, Hssh.*
  Family is replaced by stair-cohort; an Ossuin's "siblings" descended the
  same year.
- **Spindles/roostless:** given name + whatever sticks: *Ammet Threadbare,
  Brell Coinfeather.* A Spindles name with three parts means someone is
  putting on airs or hiding.

### Law and Justice <!-- id: myth-lore-574717a572d2 -->

Sky Law and the three fall-crimes; Crown, Moult, and Reef legal cultures.

## Law and Justice

Three legal cultures, one shared bedrock:

- **The shared bedrock — Sky Law**, older than the Compact, enforced
  everywhere by everyone: (1) shrine-ground is inviolate; (2) a bier-party
  passes unharmed; (3) no one cuts a tether, fouls a rain-tank, or taps
  another roost's marrow — these three are *fall-crimes*, punished by the
  Long Drop (cast down, wings bound) in every jurisdiction. Even the
  Lanhawks flog rather than shelter a tether-cutter.
- **Crown law:** written, codified, gallery-graded (a Ninth-Gallery oath
  weighs less than a Second's in court — reformers hate this; it persists).
  Punishments run to fines, gallery demotion, archive erasure (civil death),
  and for sedition the Quiet Cells, which are exactly what they sound like.
- **Moult law:** restitution, not punishment. Every wrong is priced in
  lane-favors by the raft-council; what cannot be repaid is worked off. The
  unpayable wrong is exile — "given to the open sky" — which the Vael
  consider a death sentence with extra steps and the Crown keeps mistaking
  for leniency.
- **Reef law:** the contract is god; the arbiter is whoever both parties'
  charters name; the appeal is weather.

### Money and Daily Economy <!-- id: myth-lore-185805705e9f -->

Lacquer-scrip, lane-favors, mist-pearls; wages, prices, and what people actually eat.

## Money and Daily Economy

- **Lacquer-scrip** (Crown): bone chits sealed in numbered lacquer; good
  wherever the Crown's credit is. Daily wage, dock-hand: ~2 scrip. A good
  glider: 300. A steel knife: a year's wages.
- **Lane-favors** (Moult): a ledgered debt of carriage — "one parcel, one
  passenger, one season's priority." The Vael economy is favors all the way
  down; scrip is for strangers.
- **Mist-pearls** (everywhere, uneasily): the high-value store. Pearls
  don't inflate, don't rot, and are only made by the Deepway, which prices
  them in services rendered to the dead. Carrying many marks you as rich,
  pious, or a robber of one of the two.
- Most folk eat skyweed cakes, krill-paste, eggs, and roost-fowl; drink
  rain, weed-beer, and (if Vael) fermented krill-brine that outsiders
  describe with their whole face. Brightgyre grain is wealth; real fruit is
  legend and festival.

### Living on a Spire <!-- id: myth-lore-52d6e5f2693c -->

The vertical city: crown to root-line, flight-wells, roosts, and who sleeps where.

## Living on a Spire

A settled spire is a vertical city: crown (watch-roosts, beacons, the
wealthy's wind-gardens), galleries (carved ring-streets, workshops,
markets), waist (the great middle warren of homes), root-line (docks for
barges, Marrower works, the descent-stations), all stitched by flight-wells
— vertical shafts with right-of-way rules fledglings learn before grammar
("rise on the sunward side; sink on the shade").

Homes are **roosts**: bone-carved rooms with perch-lofts, woven screens, and
always — law and custom both — two exits. Doors are for weather, not
privacy; privacy is made with screens and with the elaborate Alar etiquette
of *not having heard things*. Fledglings sleep in clutch-lofts; elders keep
the lowest, warmest rooms, closest to the level they'll leave by.

### Etiquette Worth Knowing <!-- id: myth-lore-df4c1b2a0472 -->

Greetings, gifts of lift, primaries, singing, speaking of the dead, and 'may your air move'.

## Etiquette Worth Knowing

- Greet by showing both palms then folding the wings full shut: "I am not
  landing on you; I am staying." Wings half-open during conversation
  signals hurry or distrust.
- Gifts of *lift* (a flask of marrowgas, a wing-mend, paid passage)
  outrank gifts of goods.
- Never touch another's primaries. Wing-grooming is for kin, lovers, and
  surgeons.
- Whistling indoors is rude (it mimics shear-wind); singing is always
  permitted; a roost that bans singing is hiding something, ask any Vael.
- The dead are spoken of in the present tense until they have been carried
  down, then in the past. Getting this wrong is the fastest way to clear a
  room.
- "May your air move" is the universal parting. Its inversion — *still air
  find you* — is the worst thing one Alar can say to another, and in the
  last decade it has stopped being said at all, even in anger.

### Character Creation and Roleplay Guide <!-- id: myth-lore-244d1593afaa -->

The full table procedure: concept, characteristics, culture, career, combat style, Windworking, organizations, passions, roleplay guidelines, development, and company frames.

# CHARACTER CREATION & ROLEPLAY GUIDE
### Making an Alar of the Veilwrack (Mythras Imperative)

*A complete table procedure. Mechanics follow the Mythras Imperative SRD
with the Veilwrack adaptations from alar-options.md; this document is the
player-facing walkthrough plus the roleplay layer — passions, upbringing,
and development.*

---

## Step 0 — Concept, in one sentence

Before dice: *"A ___ from ___ who wants ___ and fears ___."* The setting
pulls hardest on characters with (a) a reason to fly toward trouble and
(b) someone or somewhere they cannot bear to lose. The Gale Wardens are the
default frame — assume your character has just joined, or is about to.

With the GM, pick your **upbringing** and read your page of
upbringings.md ("What my Mother/Father/Priest/Chief told me"). That
document is what your character *believes*. You start the campaign inside
one of those four worldviews; the campaign is partly about what happens
to it.

## Step 1 — Characteristics

Roll STR, CON, DEX, POW, CHA on **3d6**; INT and SIZ on **2d6+6** (or
point-build by table agreement). Then apply the Alar frame: **SIZ −3
(minimum 4), DEX +2.**

Kindred adjustments and bonus (choose one kindred):

| Kindred | Adjustment | Kindred bonus |
|---|---|---|
| **Vael** (kestrel-kin) | −1 SIZ further, +1 DEX | +5 to one DEX-based skill |
| **Roak** (corvid-kin) | — | +5 to one INT-based skill |
| **Ossuin** (condor-kin) | +2 SIZ | +5 Endurance **or** Willpower |

Derive attributes as standard Mythras (the CLI does this automatically:
`create-character --roll` or `--stats`): Action Points, Damage Modifier,
Experience Modifier, Healing Rate, Luck Points, Magic Points = POW,
Initiative Bonus. Movement: **4m walking / 12m flying** (see Aerial
Movement rules for gaits, altitude, and carrying).

## Step 2 — Culture

Your culture is *where* you fledged, not your kindred (a Roak raised on
the Moult is Nomadic). It sets your Standard-skill bonuses and Professional
options per the SRD:

- **Nomadic** (the Moult, courier families, gleaner-bands)
- **Civilized** (Suruveil's Crown, Brightgyre, the big spire-towns)
- **Barbarian** (Carrowspar, the lesser roosts, the Spindles, the Reefs)

Spend **100 points** among your cultural skills (max +15 to any one at
creation). Every Alar culture includes **Flight (STR+DEX)** as a Standard
skill. No Alar culture grants Swim.

## Step 3 — Career

Spend **100 points** among your career skills (same +15 cap). The ten
setting careers are in alar-options.md — Lane Courier, Gale Warden, Quill,
Wind-Pilot, Death-Diver, Marrow-Miner, Shrine-Cantor, Windworker, Crown
Agent, and the SRD's generic careers re-skinned freely. Your career also
hands you a starting organization membership (Step 6) and your kit.

## Step 4 — Bonus points & combat style

Spend **150 bonus points** anywhere (career, culture, or any Standard
skill; same cap), **or** use the Skill Pyramid for a more dramatic spread:
one skill +50, two +40, three +30, four +20, five +10.

Take **one combat style** at STR+DEX, improved like any skill. Pick from
the six Veilwrack styles in alar-options.md (each carries an SRD trait —
e.g. *Warden Skirmisher* for sky-rescue fighting, *Stoopwing* for diving
attacks) or name your own with the GM and pick a fitting trait. Equipment
per career: bone, horn, lacquer, and sinew weapons are standard; steel is
heirloom-tier; armor is torso-and-head only (wings are always bare — see
the anatomy lore).

## Step 5 — Windworking (optional)

If your concept is a Windworker, take the **Magic (POW+CHA)** Professional
skill via culture/career/bonus points and roll **1d4+1 starting spells**
from the Windworking list (alar-options.md). Casting economy, traits, and
learning rules are the SRD Magic system (rules/magic.md). Remember the two
setting facts every Windworker knows in their feathers: the Breath answers
*slowly* these days — and in a Stilling it does not answer at all.

## Step 6 — Organization

Choose one membership at entry rank from organizations.md (Gale Wardens,
Quillate, Deepway lay-bearer, Cantorate wick, Featherwrights, Combine,
even — bravely — the Choir's outer ring). Write down: your rank, your
sponsor's name (make one up with the GM), and **one thing the organization
expects of you that you'd rather it didn't.**

## Step 7 — Passions

Take **three passions**: one at 40+POW+CHA, one at 30+POW+CHA, one at
20+POW+CHA. Passions augment skills (+1/5th of the passion, via the CLI's
`--augment`), can be rolled to resist compulsion or temptation, and are
the GM's invitation list for your subplots. Build each from a different
row:

| Row | Pattern | Veilwrack examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Loyalty** (person/company) | Loyalty to ___ | wing-sister; Commander Esk; my stair-cohort; the rafts of my mother's tether |
| **Love/Protect** (place/people) | Protect ___ | Lanternfall's lamp-children; the Spindles nets; my home shrine; the Moult |
| **Duty/Creed** (belief) | Uphold ___ | the lane-songs; the funeral right; the completeness of the Record; the guild oath ("no one falls") |
| **Hate/Fear** (shadow) | Hate/Fear ___ | the still air; the Combine; tether-cutters; what my lineage is hiding |
| **Yearning** (want) | ___ | to kiss the Glare; to read the sealed stacks; to hear what the divers heard; a deed-name worth keeping |

**Rule of thumb:** at least one passion that binds you to another PC or to
the company, and at least one that will someday point *against* the
mission. The campaign's decision points are built to pull passions in two
directions; a character whose passions can't conflict is a character the
finale can't reach.

## Step 8 — Finishing touches

- **Backstory:** answer your upbringing-page's four questions *in your own
  character's words* — one paragraph total is plenty. Give the GM one name
  from your past (kin, rival, debt) — it will come back.
- **The keepsake:** every Alar carries one object that doesn't appear on
  any equipment list. Name it. (A primary feather from a dead sister; a
  lane-favor chit, uncashed for nine years; a mist-pearl you shouldn't
  have.)
- **Persist it:** the GM records the finished character with
  `create-character --campaign <id> --narrative "<backstory>"` — the
  narrative field is your save file; write it like you'll want it back in
  six months, because you will.

## Roleplay Guidelines

- **Fly like it costs something.** Flight is freedom, fatigue, and risk in
  one. Say *how* you fly — riding the well, stooping, laboring under cargo
  — and watch your Fatigue.
- **Grounded is vulnerable.** An Alar with a wounded wing plays it like a
  sighted person gone blind: Grounded Dread is in the racial abilities for
  a reason. Don't shrug it off; mine it.
- **Sound is safety.** Alar culture is loud — drumming roosts, sung
  schedules, whistled right-of-way. Quiet places make your character's
  hackles rise *before* the player knows why. Use that instinct; it is
  usually right.
- **Believe your catechism.** Play your upbringing's answers as true until
  the world breaks them. The campaign's revelations land hardest on
  characters who genuinely believed their mother.
- **Honor the three irreversibilities.** Wing-loss, the Long Drop, and the
  Quieting are the setting's permanent stakes. The table should treat all
  three with table-wide gravity — no comedy Quieting, ever.

## Development & Downtime

Experience Rolls per the SRD (typically 1–3 per session; the CLI tracks
them). Between sessions, downtime maps to the setting:

- **Training** with your organization (◆ skills cheaper; new spells need a
  teacher, 3 Experience Rolls, and a week).
- **The Patching** (Long Calm season): repair gear, mend relationships,
  raise a passion by 1d10 if you spent the season honoring it — or lower
  one you betrayed.
- **Rank advancement** when the fiction supports it (see organizations.md
  — promotions are story events, not purchases).
- **Wealth** arrives as scrip, favors, and obligations, rarely as
  treasure. The best loot in the Veilwrack is *access*: a chart, a
  witness-mark, a song, a name.

## Sample Company Frames (pick one, or improvise)

1. **The Lanternfall Draft** (default): you are the Warden flight assigned
   to the dying lane — a courier, a fighter, a healer/priest, a scholar or
   Windworker. Built-in patron, mission, and first session.
2. **The Survey:** a Quillate-funded mixed company re-charting a retired
   lane that has started, impossibly, to sing again.
3. **The Bier-Party:** Deepway lay-bearers and hired wings carrying an
   important body to Carrowspar — through pirate sky, with something in
   the wax that three factions want.
